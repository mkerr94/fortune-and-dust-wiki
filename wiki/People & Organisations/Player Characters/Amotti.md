# Backstory

To survive in the wilds of the Underdark (the Drow homeland), one must not delay when the Spider Queen (Lolth demon goddess) bestows her gifts. For with even the slightest hesitation, the briefest moment of doubt, the Flesh-Carver will snatch back her blessing, depriving her chosen of the wonders of her favour.

However, this was not the case for Amotti who was born into a prominent drow house (Xai) in the depths of the Underdark. Amotti and his twin sister [[Maeve]] were groomed from birth to serve the Spider Queen, Lolth and were inseparable in their youth. However, as they grew older, a rift emerged between the siblings. While Amotti questioned the cruelty and darkness of their society, Maeve embraced it wholeheartedly, becoming a loyal servant of the Flesh-Carver Lolth.

**The Twilight Keepers**

As Amotti journeyed deeper into the darkness, he found in small corner of the underdark where a group of drow known as the ‘Twilight Keepers’ (also referred to as ‘The Keepers’)call home. The leader of the group (Vel) spoke to Amotti of the teachings of Eilistraee, the moonlit goddess of song and dance and over time he found solace in her guiding light amidst the shadows of the Underdark. Over many years Vel became like a father to Amotti. 

The keeper Vel in his teachings, emphasised the importance of balance and harmony, acknowledging the inherent darkness within their souls while striving to embrace the light. They preach of redemption and forgiveness, urging their followers to seek absolution for past sins while embracing the hope of a brighter future.

Yet, amidst their message of enlightenment and unity, the Twilight Keepers do not shy away from the darker aspects of their faith. They acknowledge the power and influence of Lolth, the Spider Queen, teaching that to deny her existence is to deny a part of themselves. Through rituals of blood and sacrifice, they seek to harness the dark energies of both goddesses, wielding them as a weapon against their enemies. Vel had taught Amotti on how to draw on the twilight and use the powers from both Lolth and Eilstraee. 

Amotti took to wearing distinct earrings representing the duality of his beliefs and the twilight balance. One earing was a dark and menacing symbol depicting Lolth (a spider). On the other side, hangs the symbol of Eilistraee, a stark contrast to the sinister emblem of Lolth. A delicate circle representing the moon, with a graceful sword piercing through it. When Amotti casts dark magic his eyes and the earing of Lolth burn red and when Amotti casts light magic his eyes and the Eilistraee earing beam blue. When used together the red and blue intertwin to create a twilight purple.

The Twilight Keepers embrace both the light and the dark, the good and the evil, in a delicate dance of balance and defiance.

**Twilight Keepers Prayer:**

In twilight's embrace, we find our place. Thriving in shadows, striving in light's embrace. For harmony's sake both light and dark we bear. Ensuring the balance with sacrificial care. FOR THE BALANCE

**The Decision**

House Xai had began a merciless campaign of conquest against a neighbouring settlement of surface dwellers and was slaughtering every man, woman and child with Maeve leading the charge. Amotti could feel the dark beginning to outweigh the light. Therefore, Amotti faced a harrowing choice, could he restore balance by betraying his family? Feeling lost and desperate, he prayed both Lolth and Eilistraee, for guidance and Lolth reached whispering dark promises of power and salvation in exchange for a terrible sacrifice.

**The Sacrifice**

To fulfil Lolth's demands, Amotti was instructed to sacrifice his beloved teacher, Vel, a mentor who had guided him through the darkness of the Underdark. Despite his inner turmoil, Amotti felt compelled to heed Lolth's words, believing that only through such a sacrifice could he achieve the greater good. With a heavy heart, Amotti had decided to betray Vel, offering him up as a pawn in Lolth's sinister game.

**The Lesser Evil?**

In a cold suffocating cavern deep in the underdark Amotti met up with his mentor…his father. Amotti faced his mentor, Vel, with a heavy heart. He looked him in the eyes and in a  coldtone whispered “for the balance” and with a trembling hand, he wielded his blade, sacrificing Vel for the greater good. Vel's cry echoed, his eyes pleading for mercy. But Amotti knew mercy was a luxury he couldn't afford. As Vel fell, Amotti sank to his knees, his soul burdened by the irreversible deed done in the name of the Spider Queen and the lesser evil.
