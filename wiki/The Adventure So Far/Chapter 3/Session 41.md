# The Events of Session 41

The wagon trundles through the prairie behind [[Fortuna Outpost]], leaving the events of the past few days behind. [[Silas Seer|Silas]] rests his head on his arms as he looks back, quietly contemplating an old life taken from him and an uncertain road ahead. The Players begin to discuss [[Maeve]], and her attack on the outpost. [[Wild Bill]] and [[Amotti]] debate whether or not Maeve was there solely to pursue and exact revenge on her brother, and what the implications of this mean with regard to Amotti's share of the blame for what happened. Amotti protests that Maeve's obsession with him does not mean he bears any guilt for her actions, but Bill disagrees.

> *"Amotti, listen here feller. If my father were to turn up right now and start trying to kill us, I wouldn’t suddenly think to myself 'damn, that's a weird coincidence!'. The simple fact remains that, if you weren't with us, she wouldn't have attacked us."*

Amotti argues that he has acted only in the interests of the group, and has saved them on several occasions. He explains that his worldview is one of balance between dark and light, something he always strives for, and something that Maeve has forgotten. He then answers the unspoken question on each of the group member's minds.

> *" Still… even now… if it came to it… I'm not sure I could do what needs to be done."*

Silas asks if, despite everything, Amotti still loves his sister. The group fall silent for a spell, his question answered only by the rhythmic rumblings of the wagon and the gentle rustle of the prairie grass around them. Conversation turns to the trail before them, and Silas explains:

> *"This trail was made when the mine was first built, before the Coal Track. The soldiers used  it to bring supplies to [[Underton]]. The path would've been watched, guarded at all times. Look at it now, since the railway, there's barely anything left. And with the mine gone silent, well… who knows what we'll find."*

Silas then asks, should they meet any of [[The Protectorate]] sent to Underton by [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias]] on the road, if any of the Players are capable of magically disguising him. The consensus is no, but [[Sel]] offers to turn Silas invisible as a last resort. Amotti also offers help, demonstrating his darkness spell, which obscures everyone's vision and blinds them and the horses to the road ahead. Silas remarks:

> *"Oh good, I feel safer already…"*

The Players travel onward as the trail winds through the prairie grass before them and the day passes, the sun cresting above them in the sky. In the low light of the early evening, they see the silhouette of a small building a mile or so up the trail. [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] decides to investigate alone, able to move and respond to potential danger quicker this way. The group decide on a signaling system using arm gestures for Eli to report his findings. Arms out-to-in means 'all fine, come here'. Arms in-to-out means 'danger, stay back'. Sel ponders the ambiguity of this system but can't voice his concerns before Eli disappears up the trail.

Eli finds that the building occupies a small clearing in the tall grass. It comprises two floors, and is constructed of wood and a corrugated iron roof. Outside, there are signs of an abandoned camp. Eli finds a central firepit containing cold and burnt-out ashes. There are hitching posts with no horses attached. An eerie stillness lies over this place, and Eli finds no signs of any remaining occupants.

His attention turns to the building itself. Circling the perimeter, he sees that the windows have been barred by hastily-secured planks of wood from the inside. One of the lower-floor rear windows has been smashed in, and through it he sees a shaft of evening sunlight illuminate the room beyond. There is a central table and chairs, all of which have been upturned. He sees deep gouge-marks in the walls and floor, along with dried bloodstains.

Confident that the building is abandoned, Eli decides to try the front door. He pushes it carefully. It swings open. Eli notices that the frame surrounding the handle has been destroyed completely. He pokes his head over the threshold and scours the interior of the building. Moving his gaze in a semi-circle from right to left, he sees: a set of stairs against the front wall leading to the upper floor, a tactical board with a map and various annotations, a rudimentary kitchen along the far wall beyond the upturned furniture; and finally a dark shape obscured by shadow in the front-left corner of the room. He voices a tentative 'hello' and is met with silence. He returns to the clearing and makes the 'arms out-to-in' gesture to the group.

Wild Bill is the first to arrive, riding Rusty Jack. He finds Eli sitting in silence on one of the logs near the firepit, crossbow pointed at the threshold of the building. Bill draws Luck, pointing her barrel in the same direction. After a few minutes of stillness, he surveys the ground around Eli, and sees signs indicating that several large things have been dragged through the dirt nearby. He follows one of these trails all the way to the edge of the long grass, where it becomes obscured. The hairs on the back of his neck stand up as the breeze rustles through the prairie grass in front of him.

The others arrive, and [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] offers to venture inside to investigate the dark shape. Using the ethereal green glow of his sword, he illuminates the figure and finds it to be the slumped body of a Protectorate soldier. There is no sign of a pulse. [[Gambit]] instructs Ralf to lift the figure's head, and to the group's horror they see that the lower jaw swings loose, having been torn from the upper by some force. Sel deduces that this was likely done by a creature with long fingers and strong limbs, as broken bottom teeth indicate the jaw was gripped between these and the chin and pulled downward with force.

The Players move upstairs. They find a single door at the top, which Caeldrid forces his way into despite it having been barred by planks of wood from the inside. In the room beyond, they see that the upper windows too have been barred. One has been smashed in from the outside and Sel observes by claw marks on the exterior wall and blood on the jagged glass at the bottom of the frame that something scaled the wall, broke its way in and then dragged its prey back out through the window. Looking around the room, Sel also sees evidence that a table and set of chairs which must have previously occupied the room have been broken and used to barricade the windows. He notices a desk against the right-hand wall, and becomes curious as to why this was not also dismantled and used for barricade material.

Eli investigates the desk, and finds a locked drawer with a key hole. Caeldrid goes to force it open but is warned by Sel that, if there is something inside, he may risk breaking it by using force. Instead, Caeldrid pries it open using his crowbar. The jolt of the drawer opening causes a round, glass bottle inside to almost ricochet and smash off of the front of the drawer but Eli catches it just in time. Holding it up to the light, he sees that it is spherical, with a thin neck stoppered by a cork and cloth. The glass itself is a deep, opaque brown and a thick liquid swirls within.

Caeldrid finds a note left behind in the drawer. Written on rough parchment, it says simply:

> *"Stay in the light."*

Caeldrid raises his eyes slowly to the broken window, and feels his stomach twist as he sees the shadows outside elongate in the swiftly-setting sun. Sel rushes to the window and shouts to his companion still frozen in place at the edge of the grass.

> *"BILL!"*
