# The Events of Session 39

Having been released from the vision by [[Silas Seer|Silas]], [[Amotti]] collapses to the floor and begins to shake. However, just as the group fear he may be stricken by some kind of psychic affliction from his interaction with Silas' powers, he recovers with a sharp intake of breath and tentatively pushes himself up from the ground. Still processing the revelations for himself, he does his best to explain to the group the truths that have just been passed on to him regarding the death of [[Ares de Vries]].

The group discuss the morality of the situation and how to proceed, now knowing that Silas intends to allow himself to be hanged for his [[The Protectorate|Protectorate]] brother's crimes in order to spare them the same fate. Silas reiterates that he is committed to his decision, despite the group's protestations. His resolve softens, however, when [[Sel]] suggests that they fake the hanging - giving [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias]] his scapegoat for retaining order in camp but allowing Silas to escape. Something akin to hope flickers in Silas' eyes.

> *"You know, as noble as all this talk of martyrdom is, I would rather like to keep my neck if it can be helped."*

[[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] is skeptical. He outlines the risks of such a plan. Josias is bitterly experienced, and [[Ghrist Skylark]] will be watching every step of the coming trial with trained eyes. One slip-up, and the consequences could be dire. He suggests invoking the ancient practice of trial by combat, and offers to stand as Silas' champion. Equinus issues a grim warning.

> *"And if Josias stands to oppose you? What then? Could you kill one innocent man to spare another?"*

The group debate the two paths before them at length. [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] loses patience and reminds the party that time is short, the swiftest and cleanest solution is to simply vote. Ultimately, the vote is tied and rests with [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]]. She votes with Sel, and the group decide to fake Silas' death.

Sel outlines his plan. He will make Caeldrid invisible, allowing him to slip past the crowd at the trial and position himself underneath the gallows. When Silas is hanged, Caeldrid will be waiting underneath to catch him. At the moment of hanging, Sel will magically create the sound of Silas' neck snapping. [[Equinus Roth|Equinus]] will then pronounce Silas dead in front of Josias and the gathered soldiers, and the rope will be cut. Silas will then drop below the gallows out of sight. Afterward, Equinus predicts that his 'body' will be taken outside of the outpost for burial - at which point Silas can make his escape. Begrudgingly agreeing to the plan, Equinus issues one final warning to Silas.

> *"You could never be yourself again, Silas. You'll sleep with one eye open each night and feel a knife at your back wherever you turn. You'd be an outlaw, brother, you understand that?"*

Sel intervenes.

> *"Better… a living… outlaw… than a dead… saint."*

Realising that the crux of their plan relies on a specific construction of the gallows, Eli asks Equinus how this will be handled. Equinus relays that [[Festus 'Anvil' Brim]] is constructing the gallows in the central yard as they speak, and Eli decides to go outside and see how this is being done.

He finds Festus busy at work, and sees to his dismay that the gallows are being constructed as a simple platform and a stool has been placed for Silas to be kicked from. Eli returns to convey this to the group, and Sel and Wild Bill go to influence Festus. They suggest to him that a hole for Silas to drop into would produce a quicker, cleaner snap - and is the least kindness that he could perform for his brother. They also stipulate for an opening to be placed at the rear of the platform, allowing Silas' body to be recovered discretely from underneath after the rope has been cut. Wild Bill decides to oversee the gallows construction while the others go to the barracks to rest, but he stays only a few hours before also retiring for the night.

In the barracks, the group observe that young [[Marius Flint]] is sitting on his bunk awake, muttering to himself. Sel and Caeldrid discuss Sel's unwillingness to risk Caeldrid in a trial by combat, and Sel expresses how invaluable he thinks Caeldrid is to the completion of their mission. Before going to sleep, Sel carves the words "The Fembots were here, S+C 4 eva" inside a love-heart shape on one of the bunk posts.

The morning arrives, still and terrible. The party make their way outside, and see that the soldiers are already gathered to await Silas. There is no sound in the air, save the cawing of the crows circling above and the gentle creak of the newly-constructed wood in the prairie breeze. With his perceptive eyes, Eli studies Josias, who stands atop the gallows alone. Although his face is fixed with stoic resolve, Eli perceives a slight tremble in his knee, belying a simmering uncertainty within.

From the lock-up, Silas is brought forth by Equinus and Skylark Ghrist. The gathered brothers hang their heads in sombre respect as he is brought before the noose and it is placed around his neck. Caeldrid, made invisible by Sel before leaving the lock-up, moves through the crowd. He accidentally brushes past an agitated Marius Flint, who for a second is distracted by this seemingly-from-nowhere contact. However, the situation is quickly averted by Wild Bill, who places a comforting hand on Marius' shoulder and engages him in a brief conversation.

Caeldrid reaches the back of the gallows, and looks up nervously at the back of Captain Josias, who is just visible over the rim of the platform. To his panic he sees that, where a simple opening was requested, Festus has constructed a door for access to the rear of the gallows. Caeldrid surmises that this will make a noticeable sound when opened, and in preparation greases the hinges with some oil from his woodcarver's tools. He waits for Josias to shout his opening address, and uses the noise to quickly open the door and slip underneath the gallows. He sees a pillar of dull light streaming in from the hole above, into which Silas is to be pushed. Caeldrid positions himself, and waits with baited breath.

Josias, charging Silas with the murder of both Ares and [[Braskin Dropstone|Braskin]], asks the onlooking crowd if anyone has anything to offer in his defence. In one last attempt to prevent Silas' death without trickery, Sel speaks up, and questions Josias on whether he has fully considered all the evidence before him and if he is certain enough to condemn a sworn brother to the noose. Josias snaps that he has heard enough from outsiders, but Eli notices a flicker of doubt in his eyes. In this moment, Silas himself speaks up.

> *"If I may, I am guilty of the crimes laid before me. I go now to face the consequences of my actions with those I have wronged. The Warrior has a plan for you all, brothers. I can see it, looking in your eyes. You are meant for greater things, some something yet to come. I am not. I ask only your forgiveness in the life to come."*

Josias narrows his eyes.

> *"Very well."*

In one swift movement, he steps forward and pushes Silas into the hole. Silas' body plunges below the gallows to an uncertain fate as the noose tightens…
