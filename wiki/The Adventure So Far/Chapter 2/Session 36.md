# The Events of Session 36

One by one, the Players awake in the [[Fortuna Outpost]] lock-up. Just as they had the previous morning. This morning. Captain [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias Bartolomeu]] informs them, just as he did before, that one of his men has been found dead. As he knows for certain that the Players were in the lock-up all night, they are now the only ones at Fortuna he can fully trust.

Wasting no time, [[Sel]] begins to try and convince Josias of their situation, that all of this has been lived before and something must be changed to prevent the tragic events to come. Joined by the others, he bolsters his arguments with details that could only be known if he was speaking the truth: the body is that of [[Ares de Vries]]; he was found hanged in the armoury by [[Festus 'Anvil' Brim]]; the names of the soldiers they could not otherwise possibly have met; the imminent arrival of [[Pastor Jeremiah]]. Josias, as if suddenly remembering something that should not have been forgotten, as if having his attention brought to something he knew in himself but should never have ignored, takes a step back. The gravity of the truth spoken by the Players comes over his eyes. He asks them what must be done.

The Players discuss [[The Infection]], and agree that before anything can be done about Ares' murder and the killer still stalking the camp that it must be halted at its source. The key will be identifying that source. [[Wild Bill]], still reeling from the previous night's betrayal and his death at the hands of [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]], questions angrily how he can trust anyone here. [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]], speaking with reason and discipline, manages to quell his fear. She encourages the rest of the group to explain one-by-one how they came to be infected and by whom they were turned. In this way, she demonstrates to Wild Bill that, now they day has begun anew, none of these events have yet come to pass. The Players have been given the gift of a second chance to halt the impending doom.

Josias swiftly and carefully escorts the Players to his quarters. There, he returns their gear and removes their [[Dimeritium]] cuffs. As he does so, Lythara catches him off guard and strikes him in the face with her cuffs. Taken aback and crying out in pain, Josias draws his sword and demands that she explain herself. Lythara swiftly apologises, and recounts how, while infected, she could no longer feel any physical pain. That Josias reacted in pain at her blow without having time to possibly fake his reaction, marks him as clear of infection. Wiping the blood from beneath his nose with the resolve of a seasoned campaigner, Josias nods his understanding.

With this knowledge in hand, the Players and the captain devise a plan. Josias will indeed summon [[Equinus Roth|Equinus]] to inspect the Players for sickness, at which point they will assess if Equinus himself is infected. From there, they will line up the remaining soldiers outside of the mess hall under the pretense of the same routine medical check. In this way, they will be able to systematically determine who is and isn't infected at this point in the day's cycle.

Equinus arrives and [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]], having once again been turned invisible by Sel in preparation, nicks him with his dagger while unseen. Equinus jolts in pain, and the Players agree that he cannot yet be infected. With Josias' backing, and allowing Equinus to sit and take in the situation, the Players explain to him their entrapment in the time distortion and the danger posed by the spreading infection. Lythara asks whether, with Equinus not yet infected, whether it can be spreading yet at all. Caeldrid and [[Gambit]] recall that they could sense infection in [[Hermus Aspall]] the previous day, and Eli points out that that they cannot say by whom he was turned. Listening to this and considering the facts carefully, Equinus replies gravely.

> *"If the source is not in this room, then it is already spreading. We must be swift, we must be thorough."*

Thinking on Hermus, Lythara remembers that [[Marius Flint|Marius]] was in possession of his bone-whistle. Equinus remarks that Hermus is never without it, and rarely leaves the dovecot tower. He would not have given it to Marius willingly. Wasting no further time, the Players enact their plan with Josias' and Equinus' help.

However, stepping out into the training yard, their plan is almost derailed by the timely arrival of Pastor Jeremiah. They swiftly bring the Pastor into the fold and listen to what he has to say. To the Players shock, Jeremiah states that he can, in fact, recall them and their adventure with [[Brez-ak]] and the dust-dwellers. He states that things appear to be worse than 'the last time he was here' indicating knowledge of the time distortion and events in camp also. Questioned about his previous disappearance from the barracks, Jeremiah responds that he 'cannot hold himself here for long'. Jeremiah goes on to explain that, whatever is happening in Fortuna, it is in defiance of The Creator and he is here to do what he can to stop it. With too many mysteries surrounding the jovial preacher, and time running short, the Players simply agree that Jeremiah's knowledge and assistance could be key to their success and accept his help. However, he must first be checked for infection.

The group round up the soldiers of Fortuna, and line them up outside of the mess hall. Caeldrid and Gambit keep watch over the line, and confirm the presence of all in the camp. The Players decide to bring in Jeremiah first, hoping to clear him as quickly as possible and bring him on-side. As Equinus feigns to inspect his eyes for sickness, Sel nicks Jeremiah with one his sharp claws. Jeremiah jolts at the sudden pain, and the group assess him as clear.

From here, one-by-one, the Players use this strategy to bring each soldier into the mess hall in turn and test them for infection. As they do so, Berstol and [[Silas Seer|Silas]] flash [[Amotti]] a strange look. One of fear. One of half-recognition, as if remembering a face from a nightmare. Systematically, the group determine who among the camp is infected and who is clear. Hermus, they confirm as already corrupted. To Josias' sadness and Equinus' regret, they also identify one other. Young Marius Flint. Marius looks nervously about, as if feeling walls closing in, and pleads to Josias that nothing is wrong with him. Josias asks Wild Bill and Gambit to escort Hermus and Marius to the lock-up for quarantine, but asks that care is taken where the boy is concerned.

Wild Bill and Gambit place an increasingly anxious Hermus and Marius into their former cell in the camp lock-up. Marius, open seeing the door close and the lock turning, begins to talk to himself. Wild Bill and Gambit look on in confusion as, increasingly erratically and fearfully, Marius pleads with an unseen party.

> *"What do I do now? No… please… not that. Just tell me what to do! No… no… NO!"*

Marius jolts back and begins to convulse. From his body, a purple flame emerges and shoots past the bars of the cell, past a stunned Wild Bill and Gambit and out into the training yard. Wild Bill runs to the mess hall to warn the others, and the Players and the uninfected soldiers gather in the yard. Above them, they see the previously bright early morning skies begin to darken, heralding the arrival of the coming shadow.

Everyone, to a man, stares at the centre of the training ground silently. Before them, there is a swirl of purple smoke, seemingly appearing from thin air. A light shines from within, a fire cold and brilliant. This fire swells in size and takes on form. A figure steps from the smoke, wreathed in purple flame. They step delicately out, poised and hovering in the centre of the room.

As if viewed through a lens slowly being focused, their features come into view. Undulating strands of thin blonde hair. Sharp cheek-bones, the mark of high birth and elven grace. Two violet eyes gleam intently, studying the Players as a cat studies a mouse beneath its paw. On the figure's head sits a dark crown, twisted and ill-formed. Its band takes the form of eight spindly limbs, each resting on the head of the bearer.

A sinister laugh fills the air. The figure in the flames turns to Amotti.

> *“Well, brother, colour me impressed. You got me. How far you have run. That wasn’t on my account, was it? You should know better than anyone there’s nowhere you can run that I won’t find you. The mistress knows, and she has plans for you.*
> 
> *Look at me now, isn’t it glorious? The Spider Queen has imbued me with all of her strength. Her hunger. And now, like a helpless insect, you’ve been caught in our web. You and your little friend. Oh yes, we know about him. Both of you, clueless, until you feel the fangs sinking into your flesh.*
> 
> *"This has been fun, this little game of toy soldiers. Each so delightfully tortured, so predictably easy to manipulate. Time now, however, for the game to end."*

Amotti, dumbfounded, feels a coldness spreading from the pit of his stomach. As if all of the blood in his veins was suddenly freezing over. Amotti's eyes cloud over. His head and arms are thrown back, and he is lifted into the air as [[Maeve]] raises her hand aloft and smiles.

> *“I will admit one thing, whatever this is, this prison you’ve created to try and hold me - it was a nice trick. I suspect though, it won’t last long after I’ve snapped your traitor neck and the mistress feasts on your insides.”*

Josias screams a call to arms to his men, and the Players ready themselves to face the Spider Queen's chosen priestess.
