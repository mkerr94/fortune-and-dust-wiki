# The Events of Session 33

[[Sel]] and [[Amotti]] leave [[Fortuna Outpost]] with [[Silas Seer|Silas]], with Amotti deciding at random to head north west and continue as far as possible - hoping to test the limits or boundaries of the temporal disturbance they find themselves in. Silas follows hesitantly, and along the way he and Sel discuss life on the frontier. Sel probes Silas about the existence of strange beings or entities in the unexplored wildernesses of [[Alcadeña]]. Silas replies that there are merely rumours, given credence only by the superstitious or those who have spent years at the outpost. At first he is dismissive, but becomes somewhat pensive and looks vaguely off into the distance, lost in thought.

> *“Some say there are things that live out there on the frontier, dark things. They hide in the dust and the wind, feeding on our doubts, our anxieties, our suffering. Drawn to it, like a shark to blood. Like I say… superstitions."*

As they continue out onto the prairie, Sel points to an unseen object in the distance and directs Amotti's attention to it. Amotti instinctively runs toward the spot pointed out by Sel, but is not followed by the others. In his absence, Sel tries to convince Silas to return to Fortuna with him, leaving Amotti behind. Silas is reluctant, reiterating the dangers of the Dustlands and explaining that he would have to answer to [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Captain Josias]] if anything were to happen to Amotti. After some discussion, Silas agrees to allow Sel to return to the outpost alone while he goes to fetch Amotti.

Meanwhile, Amotti continues north west. Failing to find anything in the spot indicated by Sel, he kneels down and fills his pockets with dirt and earth which he digs from the ground below. His attention is drawn to a strange object a few feet in front of him - a purple-grey bioluminescent mushroom giving off a faint glow. He approaches it and gives it a sniff, but can't discern any distinctive smell. He then sees another seemingly incongruous plant, a spherical bud with a rocky carapace which extends and retracts its leave at his touch. Intrigued by this, Amotti recovers it with his mage hand and takes it with him. He stands and is taken aback suddenly as he looks ahead and sees several purple-grey mushrooms which now stand above his head height. Impossibly, there are tunnel walls to his left and right, spotted with bright green lichen and moss. In front of him, he sees the dark entrance to a cavern, and ventures in.

[[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]], [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]] and [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] leave [[Ghrist Skylark]] behind in his watchtower and descend once more to the training yard. Initially heading to the barracks to try and find [[Marius Flint]], they instead see a shorter, slighter figure than the other soldiers they have encountered peering into the camp well in the middle of the yard. They approach and greet this figure, who is visibly startled and turns to face them. The Players are shocked into a moment of awkward silence when they recognise this person as the young soldier who collapsed at the doors of the Town Hall in [[Clement's Hollow]] and disappeared from its lock-up. He introduces himself as Marius Flint. Recovering their composure, the group introduce themselves to Marius. He asks indignantly if the others have asked the group to check in on him, and explains that the Captain has given him a leave of duties but that this is not his wish - he is trying to busy himself about camp and return to his work. The Players enquire about the lack of numbers manning the Outpost and Marius reveals that Captain Josias has sent the bulk of their forces to the mining town of [[Underton]], on the western frontier of Alcadeña as it is currently mapped. Most of their current remit is to protect the coal shipments making their way from Underton to [[Portumluna]] and [[Samosetia]] beyond, but production has recently stopped. Josias has sent most of his soldiers to investigate.

As Marius speaks, the Players notice that he nervously scratches at his forearm, which bears several small scratches. Marius explains that these were caused by the [[chiropterrings]] in the fort's dovecot, which can occasionally get a little restless. Seeing the group's bemusement, Marius explains that these are winged messenger creatures under the care of [[Hermus Aspall]], the camp's communications officer. He continues that he was actually on his way to see [[Equinus Roth|Equinus]] about his scratches when the Players approached him. He excuses himself and goes to move past the Players, but Caeldrid asks if they might accompany him to the stables - to which he agrees. As they leave the well, Lythara turns back to investigate it, her curiosity piqued by Marius' interest in it when they approached him. She pulls up the bucket, and finds inside a small bone-whistle on a leather strap. Marius, smiling nervously, expresses relief that it has been recovered. He explains that it belongs to Hermus, and is used in his care of the chiropterrings. Lythara tests it, and finds that it produces no sound, before returning it to Marius. Any further interaction is interrupted by the sounding of the outpost's horn, indicating the arrival of a visitor.

The gates open. First to step through is Sel, returning alone from his excursion with Amotti and Silas. Next, a familiar wagon trundles through the gates and stops in the centre of the yard. None of the onlooking soldier's seem particularly surprised at this arrival, and Marius speaks up to himself.

> *"Oh, it must be the Pastor…"*

The Players look on as the figure driving the cart disembarks, and also see [[Gambit]] and [[Wild Bill]] exiting the armoury after their conversation with [[Festus 'Anvil' Brim|Anvil]]. They recognise the driver of the cart as [[Pastor Jeremiah]], their companion on the adventure through the [[The Dust-dwellers|Dust-dwellers]] caverns. The Players are startled, however, to find that Jeremiah does not appear to recognise them. Captain Josias emerges from his quarters, and greets Jeremiah as an old acquaintance.  Jeremiah explains that he has come to the outpost, not to trade in goods as he often has in the past, but in search of a solution to a problem. He goes into the back of his wagon, and returns with one of his jars. Holding it up, he shows the Captain and the Players that the firefly inside has perished, having shrivelled up and turned black. He looks at Josias with grave concern and asks if he might speak with him.

With Jeremiah's arrival, the gathered Players seize an opportunity. They show Jeremiah and Josias [[Brez-ak]]'s staff, and use their possession of it as proof that they have met Jeremiah before. Proof that Fortuna is victim to a disturbance in time. Looking to Jeremiah for confirmation, Josias is left reeling. He takes everyone back to his quarters, making sure that the onlooking soldiers return to their posts and have not overheard the conversation. Inside Josias quarters, he becomes erratic and panicked. He struggles to comprehend the possibility that the Players claims are true, and that they are from a future that he has yet to live. This is compounded when the Players tell them of Marius' death in Clement's Hollow, and Josias states that the boy must be saved from this fate. He slumps in his chair and puts his head in his hands.

Leaving the Captain to come to terms with his situation, Caeldrid, Lythara and Wild Bill decide to go to the stables to check on Marius. Eli, Gambit and Sel decide to go to the barracks to investigate Ares' former bunk. Jeremiah however, stays behind momentarily, alone with the Captain. With Josias distracted, he approaches the corpse of Ares de Vries, still on the table in Josias' quarters. From it, he removes a firefly, but finds its green glow flickering and stuttering and the creature itself frail and near death. His concern deepening, he leaves to join the party going to investigate the barracks.

At the stables, Caeldrid, Wild Bill, and Lythara find Marius assisting Equinus with mucking-out duties. Wild Bill uses Lythara to try and provoke a reaction from Marius, using some of the words from his cryptic outburst in the Clement's Hollow lock-up. Making a show of beckoning to Lythara, he repeats.

> *"She sees us, she sees us."*

Caeldrid watches Marius carefully as this happens, and sees him stiffen and tighten his grip on the pitchfork he was using to clear the stables, before relaxing slightly and putting it aside.

Equinus asks the Player's business, and they state that the Captain has requested to see Marius. Equinus grumbles about being left to clean up alone, but nods to Marius to go with a gruff but affectionate:

> *"Go on then, lad, fck off"*

As recompense for stealing Marius away, Caeldrid offers to stay behind with Equinus to help with the stables. At first poking fun that this duty might be above someone resplendent in their full plate armour, Equinus accepts. Wild Bill and Lythara take Marius, leaving Equinus and Caeldrid behind.

Meanwhile, Sel, Eli, Gambit and Jeremiah enter the outpost barracks. Inside, they find two rows of regularly spaced bunkbeds. They quickly realise there is no way of discerning which belonged to Ares, but after investigation Eli is able to identify which one it is. He deduces this by seeing that all of the beds are neatly made, save for one, whose occupant was not there to make it in the morning. He and Sel look around Ares' bed. In his trunk, they find merely some spare clothes and belongings. However, Eli spots something stuck between two floorboards under the bed, almost imperceptible expect to the keenly observant eye. He retrieves it, and finds that it is a crumpled note, handwritten in charcoal.

> *"We must discuss. The armoury. Tonight. - Telemachus."*

The group look to each other, and wonder aloud how such a note could be sent from someone they know to be dead.

Amotti ventures deeper into the mysterious cavern. Passing through damp walls lined with bioluminescent blue lampenflora, he reaches a central chamber. It is lit by a single shaft of moonlight. At its heart, a lone, hooded figure stands at an altar, poring over a large and ancient-looking book. He raises his head and reveals himself to Amotti. Amotti is shocked to see that it is his old mentor and dear companion, [[Vel]]. The old Drow, wizened with wispy grey hair and wrinkled features, addresses Amotti.

> *"The light is fading, Amotti. The balance is being tipped. Only in twilight can our people have a future. The dusk at the parting of the day and the blooming of the night. The lines have already been drawn, and the time approaches. Moonlight guide you, young one."*

Vel steps from behind the altar and approaches Amotti, who greets him tearfully. As Vel moves closer, Amotti is horrified as he sees blood begin to pool in Vel's robes at the chest. The old Drow collapses into Amotti's arms as Amotti protests.

> *"No, no, no… I can save you… I could have saved you…"*

As Vel's eyes turn black and his head falls limp, Amotti's vision clouds over and all fades around him…
