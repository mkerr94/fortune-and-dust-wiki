# The Events of Session 31

Shackled and confined in the [[Fortuna Outpost]] lock-up, the Players consider their next move. They look around, and find they have been placed in a large corner cell surrounded on two sides by thick iron bars. Through these, they see that the other cells are unoccupied at present. 

[[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] studies his shackles. He sees that they are comprised of two circular bonds with very slight seams, joined by a static metal bar in the middle. As he turns them in the lamp-light, he sees that the metal gives off a strange blue sheen. His investigations pique the interest of one of his captors, the lilac-skinned Tiefling, [[Silas Seer]]. Silas approaches the cell and remarks on the nature of the restraints. 

> *“Rather impressive, aren’t they? [[Dimeritium]] alloy. They mine it in [[the Whispering Mountains]] outside the Capital, I’m told. Handy for suppressing the skills of mage-born, and for keeping mage-craft the sole remit of the very rich. I wouldn’t force them too much, if I were you, we have arrows crafted from the same alloy and Skylark rarely misses his mark.”* 

[[Gambit]] questions why mage-craft is so actively restricted in [[Alcadeña]]. Silas explains that, in his opinion, it is a mechanism of maintaining power among the elite. 

> *“Mage-craft is power, you see, and whoever controls the power controls… well everything, really.”* 

His curiosity and appetite for stimulating conversation aroused, Silas engages further with the Players while his Goliath companion, [[Braskin Dropstone]], sits quietly at the guard’s table. Silas asks Eli and [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]] about the state-of-affairs in [[Clement's Hollow]]. The Players express that the town has been saved, for now, from its curse, which strikes Silas as odd given that the request for aid from [[Magnolia 'Old Mags' Aurix|Old Mags]] arrived in Fortuna just that same morning. Eli insists that this is evidence of a temporal anomaly affecting Fortuna and a flicker of doubt crosses Silas’ face before he dismisses the idea. 

> *“I wouldn’t go blabbing about that too persistently, my friend. It won’t help your case when Equinus comes to inspect you for sickness.”*  

Lythara asks Silas about how Mags’ letter was able to reach Fortuna, unwittingly revealing the Players relative recency in Alcadeña to him. Silas turns to her and his demeanor changes to one of light flirtation. 

> *“Hmm… you haven’t been here for very long at all, have you? We received it by chiropterring of course. Tell me, how long has it been since you arrived from the homeland? You must have passed through the Harbour of the Moon, no? Did you see it? The island and the stone fortress that sits atop it? [[Orsobia]]… I was born there you know.”* 

He and Lythara continue to discuss Silas’ personal history, and the Players passage through [[Portumluna]]. Silas explains that his father was a guard on the island prison of Orsobia, and his mother a weaver from Portumluna. When questioned about what reason Mags could have for sending a letter for aid and the Players to Fortuna, Lythara reiterates that they were not sent by Mags – they have arrived after saving Clement’s Hollow and are victim to some distortion in time. Almost overcome by his curiosity, Silas extends a hand toward Eli, but is stopped by Braskin. At this, he ends their interaction and returns to the table. 

> *“I suppose Equinus will decide. My best advice? Get some rest, and wait until morning.”* 

One by one, the Players drop off to sleep. Lythara, looking vacantly at the soft candlelight on the guard’s table, feels her eyelids droop as she enters her meditative state. In her last conscious moments, she sees a sinister figure standing in a shadowy corner of the lock-up. The figure is female, with elven features and dark blue-grey skin. She holds a crossbow. She stares at Lythara with glowing, pupilless light blue eyes and has a symbol of the same colour on her forehead. On the wall behind her, a sigil consisting of three crossed daggers appears on the wall. Lythara is unable to respond as she falls into a deep meditation, but sees that the figure disappears in a flicker of candlelight, with Silas shifting uncomfortably in his seat as they do. 

The Players awake to a soft shaft of light entering from a small, barred window above the guards table. They see that their captors are nowhere to be found. Instead, a single figure sits at the guard’s table, illuminated by the faint light. He stares into the wooden surface as if lost in thought, then lifts his head to look at the Players. It is [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu]]. The captain stands and places a key into the lock of the Players cell. Before turning it, he gives them a grave look. 

> *“Please, take this as a sign of mutual respect. I am left with no other choice. You are now the only ones I can trust. You see, one of my men… is dead. They found him, hanged, in the armoury this morning. You were locked in this cell all night, the only ones who could have played no part. Please, venga, come with me.”*  

Josias leads the Players out of the lock-up, still bound by their shackles. Adopting an air of authority, he stands tall and marches across the training yard to his quarters with the Players in tow. They are eyed suspiciously by those soldier’s present in the yard and long the perimeter walls. Eli and [[Sel]] look around. They see Silas training in the yard with the olive-skinned watchman and the green-scaled reptilian soldier from the night before. Along the walls, Eli spots the blonde half-elven soldier patrolling with Braskin the goliath. Raising his gaze upwards, he sees a strange figure leaning out of one of the corner watchtowers. The figure is bird-like in nature, with flecked grey and white feathers. He has piercing eyes and a razor-sharp beak. Despite the distance between them, he meets Eli’s gaze immediately and the two lock eyes for a moment.  

The Players enter the Captain’s Quarters. Josias uses a strange, hexagonal, metal disc to release them from their shackles. He directs their attention to a body which has been laid out on a table in the right-hand side alcove. It is the tall, muscular soldier with icy blue eyes and braided grey hair whom the Players saw leading the training session in the yard the previous evening. Josias names him as [[Ares de Vries]]. Saying nothing further for fear of biasing the Players initial investigations, he asks them to inspect the remains. Sel begins ritual casting detect magic to look for traces of mage-craft. Josias initially protests, but then begrudgingly accepts this use of mage-craft, urging the Players to only do so in private and when absolutely necessary. He intends to explain their freedom and presence around camp to the other soldiers, but he will not be able to pardon the illicit use of mage-craft by the Players without arousing suspicion from the platoon. Sel finds a low thrum of Dunamancy magic that permeates all around them, but is unable to demonstrate concrete proof of this to Josias. 

Eli cuts the clothes from Ares’ body and he and Gambit inspect the corpse. They find several things of note. There are lesions around the neck and burst blood vessels in the eyes consistent with the strain of strangulation by rope. However, Gambit finds faint purple-blue bruising marks around Ares arms and scratch arms around his neck which seem unusual in the circumstance of hanging oneself. He relays these findings to Josias, who confirms that these were also his findings. He shows Gambit that, under Ares’ nails, are traces of dried blood and skin – indicating that he clawed at his own neck as he was hanged.  

Lythara then describes her vision from the night before to the group and draws the symbol she saw on the wall for everyone to see. To her shock, Josias recognizes it as the symbol of the Forak-Erach-Naek, the Empty-scabbard Killers. He seems puzzled, as he explains that they are an elite group of psionic assassins. Their sigil is well known among those bound to the law, however they are not known to have a presence in Alcadeña and are typically linked to high-profile killings of public figures where the victim was stabbed by dagger. [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] however, expresses his concern that the connection should not be readily dismissed as coincidence.  

The Players ask about Ares’ relationship with the rest of the soldiers. Josias admits that it was strained. He recounts that, a few weeks prior to the Player’s arrival, one of their number was lost on a raid of the [[The Dust-dwellers|Dust-dweller]]’s cavern. The soldier’s name was [[Telemachus ‘Mackie’ Flint|Telemachus Flint]], known to the others as Mackie. During the raid, he and Ares were lost together in the cave network. Only Ares returned. Ares claimed that Mackie was taken by the creatures while trying to save him. His comrades have been mistrusting of him ever since. Josias reiterates to the group that, despite his unpopularity, Ares was a sworn soldier of [[The Protectorate]] and he will not believe that there was any foul play involved. He retrieves [[The Soldier's Locket]] found by Sel in the Dust-dweller’s cave, and confirms that this belonged to Telemachus.

Lamenting that he now has no other choice, Josias asks the Players to help him investigate Ares murder. He stipulates that, in order to avoid widespread panic or a witch hunt breaking out among the outpost, and to prevent the risk of the killer trying to flee or doing something rash if spooked, the other soldiers should not be told that Ares’ death is being treated as suspicious. If they are to successfully flush out the killer in their ranks, the Players must gain the trust of those they are investigating, and act with the utmost discretion. He says that he intends to call Equinus Roth, the camp medic, to inspect them for traces of sickness. If they are cleared, he will inform the others that there has been a misunderstanding. The Players are here on Rail Company business and should be allowed to go free. 

What Josias doesn’t know however, is what to tell his men about the Player’s purpose in staying at Fortuna and integrating themselves among the soldiers there. Sel suggests that he say they have been hired to provide reinforcement for the diminished numbers at Fortuna. Josias considers this agreeable. It is backed by the truth that the bulk of his forces are indeed currently elsewhere, though he does not say where. It will also provide rationale for returning the Players weapons (however this will not plausibly extend to allowing their use of mage-craft). This agreed, Josias departs to send for Equinus, and the Players prepare to catch a killer.
