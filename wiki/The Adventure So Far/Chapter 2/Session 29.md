# The Events of Session 29

The Players stand over the broken body of [[Brez-ak]] in the dust-dwellers cave. The [[The Dust-dwellers|Dust-dwellers]], having finished with Brez-ak, scuttle off into the cave and disappear into unseen crevices and recesses in its walls. [[Sel]] uses his mage-hand to lift Brez-ak’s staff, and recovers it for himself, which [[Gambit]] later identifies as the [[Staff of Water Binding]]. Solemnly and with a barely-concealed grief, [[Pastor Jeremiah|Jeremiah]] kneels at the side of Brez-ak’s corpse. From his open mouth, he draws a single firefly, glowing green as it rises into the cave. He softly places it in one of his jars, and laments a life wasted and the corruption of an old friend. He warns the Players that the dust-dwellers may return, and that they had best be on their way. He promises to lead them safely out of the canyon network.  

Returning to the crossroads, the Players regroup with [[Wild Bill]] and [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]] and explain the events in the dust-dweller’s cave. Reuniting with his horse, Charon, Jeremiah appears to forget the task at hand momentarily – having to be prodded by Lythara to show them the way out of the canyons. Cheerfully, he indicates that it is down the path that they have not yet taken to the left of the crossroads, though Lythara struggles to tell whether he knows this for sure or has simply used process of elimination to make this choice.  

Down this canyon path, the Players find the walls covered in once-strong but now withering vines. They coalesce around a faded and worn shrine carved into the wall, which Jeremiah stops his wagon to go and pray at. Following him, Lythara finds that the shrine consists of a stone basin being fed from a steady trickle of water coming from a hole in the rock face. Above the basin, a fading carving depicts the symbol of the entwined catfish first shown to the Players by [[Rel-se]]. Imitating Jeremiah, Lythara brushes her forehead with some of the shrine’s water, and feels a comfort wash over her – the reassurance that she is merely one part of a much larger and much older plan, and that this path will carry her where she is needed to go. All of the Players save for [[Amotti]] follow suit.  

The Players continue their journey along the canyon path, and Lythara and Amotti join Jeremiah on his wagon. Despite a warning from Lythara, Amotti slips into the canvas-covered carriage behind him. He finds the interior glowing with an eerie green, and looks covetously at the shelves lined with firefly jars. He attempts to take one of them and conceal it about his person. He turns to see Jeremiah poking his head through the entrance. Jeremiah, losing some of his jolly façade and becoming something altogether more otherworldly, more knowing, observes Amotti coolly and asks that he returns the jar. Amotti questions the nature of the fireflies, and pushes Jeremiah to explain why he collects them. Jeremiah states that those he collects are simply lost, and that he feels bound to show them the way. When Amotti presses further, Jeremiah smiles warmly and tries to placate him by explaining that there are some things beyond all of our understanding.  

Amotti returns the jar to the shelf, however in doing so uses his mage-hand to attempt to sneak another from the shelf behind him without Jeremiah’s notice. He feels a cold streak through his veins and fins that he cannot move. His vision blurs and is reduced to simple shapes and shadows moving before his eyes. He sees a green glow, and feels a firefly of his own emerge from his open mouth and float into the wagon – seemingly tethered by his condensing breath. With his vision fading, he feels a warm light on his shoulder, and sees the hand of Jeremiah gently caress the firefly back into his body. He comes to, and the Pastor looks on him kindly. 

> *“One day, Amotti. You too will find your way.”*

Greatly troubled by this experience, Amotti returns with Jeremiah to the front of the wagon.  

Finally, the path in front of the Players widens, and they emerge out onto an open plain in the light of the late-afternoon sun cresting overhead. Jeremiah explains that this is where they must part ways, and that his business takes him to the Almario river 30 miles or so to the South. He tells them that, if they still wish to make their way to Fortuna, they can do so by heading a few miles directly West. However, he issues a warning that he does not feel anything good coming from the place. Bidding farewell to Jeremiah, the Players set out regardless. 

Passing through largely featureless prairie, they suddenly come to a huge, swirling Dust-storm before them. They feel the rush of wind in their ears, and the grit of dust and sand between their teeth. Approaching, they see that the maelstrom stretches for miles to the north and south, and towers into the sky above. Endlessly swirling in a cyclical tempest, the winds pick up dust and sand and rock and creating a seemingly impenetrable storm-barrier before the Players.  

Steeling himself against the wind, Sel pushes forward alone. Wild Bill watches him as he goes, and fins his eyes drawn into the storm. For a split second, he thinks he sees the silhouette of a structure through a brief parting in the clouds, but is distracted by the moving of dark shapes from within the maelstrom. He sees two shadows peel from the swirling stormfront, and gracefully swooping toward Sel. Instinctively, he cries out and fires two shots from Luck into one of the creatures which causes them both to recoil.  

The creatures descend on the Players. Ten to fifteen feet in wingspan, they have scythe-like appendages on the ends of their tails. Angular yet featureless heads crest their bodies, with the centres of their abdomens dominated by a chest cavity resembling a beak which opens and shuts as they hunt. The creatures seem fixated on Sel. Swooping and circling, again and again they come at him in a frenzy and try to gain purchase on his shell. The group watch on as one picks Sel up, carrying him fifteen feet into the air before dropping him to be picked off by the others. Lythara looses her psionic arrows, [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] fires his crossbow, Gambit shoots his revolver, all of the Players attempt to drive the creatures off of their relentless pursuit of Sel.  

As one descends, [[Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] leaps off of Sel’s back and wrestles it to the ground. Eli and Wild Bill approach and finish it while it is restrained. Another, emerging from [[the Dust-storm]], is disintegrated by the blinding light of Amotti’s moonbeam. Sel attempts to flee, summoning the blue light of his Chronurgy magic. At this, more of the creatures appear from within the Dust-storm and being circling and swooping with the others. 

The Players are overwhelmed. Sel is peppered with attacks and bloodied into unconsciousness. Eli, realising flight is their only recourse, runs head first into the awaiting storm. He bursts past Caeldrid and into the tempest, screwing his eyes shut and gritting his teeth. Stumbling to his knees, he bursts through the other side and opens his eyes. He finds himself in an open prairie, a structure some way ahead of him. He turns to find no trace of the storm behind.  

Meanwhile, Caeldrid and Wild Bill attempt to drag a bruised and battered Sel through the storm barrier after Eli. They manage to enter the maelstrom itself, which seems to distract the creatures enough for some respite. In a final push, Gambit sends Ralf careening through the dust clouds towards Sel. The steel homunculus picks him up in his arms and pushes him through the rest of the storm. Sel, dazed and buffeted, pokes his head from his shell. He finds that he is in an open prairie, and a confused Eli stands before him, staring into the empty plain behind.
