# The Events of Session 38

The Players recover in the aftermath of their climactic battle with [[Maeve]]. Following the trail of smoke left in her wake, they rush to the lock-up to check on [[Marius Flint]] and [[Hermus Aspall]] in their cell. Inside, past the bars, they see a sphere of soft grey-green light. There is a strange muffled atmosphere surrounding it, sucking in all sound and muting it.. A figure stands at the centre, emerald-green eyes, a mop of curly hair and a look of unblinking resolve. He stands between the smoke and Marius, who kneels on the floor at the back of the cell, cowering. The Players look on as the smoke twists and strikes, trying to penetrate the barrier formed by the grey-green light. The man in the centre of the light, form flickering and impermanent, holds twin hand axes aloft in front of his face - keeping the smoke at bay and away from Marius with all of his waning strength. 

A purple aura grows in the room, and [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] realises that the balance is being tipped and that Marius' protector will soon be overcome. He rushes forward, but is halted when he finds the door to the cell locked and holding fast. In frustration, he rattles the door in its frame to no avail. From behind him, [[Wild Bill]] asks him to step aside. Holding Luck in his hands and staring down the sight, Bill fires straight at the lock on the door, blasting it open. Caeldrid runs into the cell and places a gauntleted hand on the mysterious figure's shoulders. Through this, he channels Protection from Evil and Good. The man strengthens and regains solid footing. Marius, as if sensing this swing in momentum, stands and lends his sword to the struggle. With one final push, the two force back the smoke and the grey-green light swells, evaporating the last remnants of evil in a brilliant flash.

The Players blink, and open their eyes to see Marius standing at the centre of the cell, looking up at his protector - whose flickering form grows fainter.

> *"[[Telemachus ‘Mackie’ Flint|Telemachus]]…"*

While to all others, their conversation sounds as if one being held underwater, Caeldrid overhears - being inside the sphere of light. The ghostly figure speaks warmth and kindness to his Marius.

> *"I don't know what's keeping me here, little brother, I just know I don't have long. I'm so sorry for leaving you, Marius. You mustn't blame yourself for any of this. You resisted in the end, that's all that matters. It seems you have found your strength after all. [[Penny Flint|Penny]] and I will always be with you, you know that. In the breeze that carries the dust across the plains, the warmth of the afternoon sun, we'll be there. There's no pain, Marius, you see? Not anymore. This life is merely the river, what comes next is the sea."*

[[Telemachus ‘Mackie’ Flint|Telemachus]] turns to Caeldrid.

> *"Thank you, all of you, for saving him. Warrior watch over you all…"*

Telemachus closes his eyes, smiles, and a light wind picks up. The atoms of his spirit becomes dust in the prairie wind, and are delivered beyond, and beyond.

Struggling to comprehend what they have just seen, the Players attention is drawn to a growing orange light at the threshold of the lock-up, coming from the training yard. [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] looks out past the door and sees some of the surviving soldiers stood transfixed, looking up at something out of sight. He walks out into the yard, and sees that their attention is fixed on a huge, shimmering image of a clock floating in mid-air. It bathes the onlookers in warm, orange light before shattering into countless shining particles of gold, which fall like fresh snow and dissolve upon the dusty ground.

Standing in the yard, with the remaining soldiers regrouping and assisting each other around them, the Players discuss what they have just witnessed. They discuss the nature of the time distortion they have found themselves in, and reason that, if Maeve assumed it was created by them, that it was not of her doing. They ask [[Amotti]] for further explanation as to Maeve's murderous intent toward him, and question the nature of her otherworldly powers. Amotti is confused as to the reason why [[Lolth]], a goddess whom he also serves, would grant Maeve powers to be used against him. Caeldrid and Amotti also speak to Marius, asking him how Maeve first came to him, to which he begins to reply but quickly becomes defensive and walks off.

> *"She feeds on your grief, your guilt. She came to me in the night, after… I have to check on the others."*

Suddenly, the Players hear the voice of [[Ghrist Skylark]] calling from above for [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Captain Josias]]. He gestures to the westernmost perimeter walkway, and the Players follow Josias up the steps to the wall to investigate. On the wall, they find the contorted body of [[Braskin Dropstone]], the goliath watchman. Slumped over him and gently sobbing is [[Silas Seer]]. Silas turns to the Captain slowly with tears in his eyes.

> *"Captain… he… he fell defending me from those creatures… he…"*

Josias looks down at the scene before him. Silas' and the Players' gaze follow his. In Braskin's side, there is a large gash from which blood is pooling. Next to this, lying on the wooden floor where it was dropped, is Silas' scimitar. It is covered in blood. A look of panic comes over Silas and he opens his mouth to protest as Skylark and Equinus arrive behind the Players. Josias speaks softly at first, and then urgent and commanding.

> *"Take Silas to the lock up."*

Skylark and [[Equinus Roth|Equinus]] look to each other and hesitate.

> *"AHORA! Move, that's an order!"*

Silas is dragged up by his brothers and hangs his head as he is pulled away. Josias turns to leave, but Wild Bill stops him to ask if they should move Braskin's body. Josias asks that they carry it into his tent. After Josias leaves, the Players surround Braskin's corpse. Wild Bill kneels to examine it further, and sees several bite marks and evidence of purple poison creeping up his torso and neck. They Players lift the huge body of the goliath, and solemnly carry him past his brothers in the yard and into Josias' tent. The surviving soldiers look on in sorrow and Ladle places his hat over his heart and dips his head in respect.

The Players find Josias slumped in his chair, having prepared his war table for Braskin's body. The Players begin to voice their doubts about Silas' guilt when Josias silences them.

> *"I have heard enough from outsiders. I shall tell you what I see. Braskin was the only one among the men strong enough to restrain Ares. And yet, he did not possess the intelligence needed to have committed this crime undetected. Silas was his closest confidant. I believe that Braskin was going to confess, and Silas used the opportunity of this attack to silence him before he could."*

Amotti begins to protest, but Josias interjects.

> *"You may stay here tonight, as thanks for your part in the battle. Afterwards, you may leave with whatever we can reasonably provide to aid in your journey. As for Silas, on the morrow he will be tried for his crimes."*

Realising the conversation is at an impasse, Caeldrid ushers the others out of the tent. Eli heads off to the barracks to rest for the night, uninterested in pursuing matters any further. The others however, feel they must visit Silas and hear his version of events. They head to the lock-up to confront him.

Inside, they see Equinus standing at the bars of the far cell. He reaches through the bars and cups the back of Silas head in his hand, pulling his forehead close to his own in a show of support and comfort. The Players see that Silas has been shackled in Dimeritium cuffs. Equinus looks up at the Players and separates himself from Silas.

> *"What the fuck do you want?"*

Silas, speaking calmly and quietly, reassures him.

> *"It's alright, brother, let them approach."*

The Players approach the bars, and ask Silas what happened. He explains that, when Maeve's spiders attacked, they swarmed around him and Braskin. Braskin fell defending Silas from attack, but when Silas came to his aid he found that he had been corrupted. Succumbing to the poison, Braskin lashed out and tried to kill Silas, but came to in a moment of clarity. He asked Silas to end his pain, and prevent him from causing harm to others. Silas obeyed.

The Players urge Silas to protest his innocence. The lilac-skinned tiefling smiles sadly and shakes his head.

"I'm afraid I can't you see. I must be tried, for Braskin and for [[Ares de Vries|Ares]], and I must be found guilty. It is now the only way."

Equinus stands silent, arms folded and leaning against the bars of the opposite cell, looking at Silas grimly. At the mention of Ares, the Players look to each other, confused. They press Silas to explain, and he takes a step back. Equinus interjects.

> *"Be sure about this, brother…"*

Silas begins to speak.

> *"I looked into his mind, Ares. I saw what he did… I lived it…"*

> *"The Captain sent us to raid the dust-dweller's cavern. They'd been getting bolder, they couldn't be allowed to continue. It was a disaster. We reached the caves, but it was a maze, and those creatures were everywhere. I don't know when it happened, if he made it happen or if he just took the opportunity, but Ares and Mackie got separated from the rest of us."*

> *"I saw it through his eyes, you see. That's my gift, and my curse. Ares hid. He was listening for Mackie's voice, his footsteps. He cried out for help to him, luring him. He drew his sword. In those caves though, the sound comes from everywhere and those things can trick you with their voices. Mackie caught Ares out, arrived from a different tunnel and confronted him. 'Are you planning to kill me, Ares?' he was so calm. Like he already knew, like he took pity on Ares."*

> *"I felt Ares' anger, and his fear. He went for Mackie, but one of the dust-dwellers leapt out of the walls. It went for Ares, it had him, and he was terrified. I could see its teeth, rippling and contracting, trying to get at him. I could feel the piss dripping down his legs. The death-fear, I felt it. Mackie saved him. He buried one of his axes in the creatures neck. Ares eyes and his locked, and there was this moment. Just a silence, as if everything held its breath. I felt the humiliation, the rage rise in Ares cheeks. He stabbed Mackie. And then again. And again, and again. His sworn brother, the man who just saved his life, he stabbed him six times."*

> *"Mackie fell to the ground. I heard the noises of more of the dust-dwellers swarming in the tunnels. He looked up at Ares one last time. Do you know what he said? 'It's ok, brother. I will buy you time. We all must pay for our sins, and mine are many.' He grabbed Ares' sword in his hand. "One day, you will understand what it means to have this."*

> *"He saved Ares life, and he killed him for it. Worse, he came back to us and lied through his teeth. Expecting us to believe."*

With the Players digesting these revelations, Silas goes on to explain that, after that, it became harder and harder to protect Marius from Ares.

> *“My father was a bully like him, you know. Invincible. Untouchable. Until, one day, they’re not. Please, I can show you. I'm afraid, however, I will need a volunteer.”*

Amotti steps forward as Silas raises his hands and Equinus removes his Dimeritium cuffs. He warns Amotti.

> *"Careful, outsider, this is not without risk."*

Amotti nods, and allows his head to be taken in Silas' outstretched hands. The Players watch as his eyes roll in his skull and his head jolts back. Amotti experiences a vision, through Silas' eyes:

You see a soft light coming from beneath the armoury door, and feel yourself walking toward it. Braskin goes in first, pushing the door open, you step inside. Inside, all of the remaining brothers are gathered, save the Captain. [[Festus 'Anvil' Brim|Anvil]], [[Ladle]]. Cerlan, Berstol, Uster. Equinus and [[Hermus Aspall]]. They look at you with grim determination as you step to the side and take your place. Marius sits alone in the corner, head in his hands, muttering softly to himself. Braskin takes his place beside the door.

The moments tick by like hours. The machine, still pulling water and heating it, depositing it into buckets with clouds of steam, clatters away, hissing and rattling. The noise drowns out all others. Ares enters. At first, his look is one of bewilderment. And then, as if suddenly realising that the hand on your shoulder is that of Death himself, the fear comes to his eyes. The terror. Braskin grabs him by both arms, pinning him. Ares starts to weep, simpering and pleading, writhing and trying to break free.

> *'No… no… please… oh gods please no… Marius… Marius I'm sorry… tell them… please don't let them… MARIUS'*

"Ares begins to sob, and his legs go limp. He is dragged before you, and Equinus steps forward."

> *'Go on then, Silas, look into his mind and be done with it.'*

He punches Ares in the gut, and Ares barely makes a sound. He hangs his head. You approach, lift it, and place your hand on his forehead. All turns to black as you close your eyes. You open them again, and you know. You turn to Marius, he is still muttering to himself, distractedly marking the number six in tally marks on his arm in charcoal. You realise that you must have been describing your vision aloud as it happened. The boy has heard everything. You turn to the others, and nod.

Braskin grabs Ares once more, and as if shaken from a trance, he snaps back into fear. He wriggles to break free as Equinus places the noose around his neck.

> *'NO! NO! NO!’*

As the brothers pull the rope, each with their own hand placed along its length, they speak in unison.

> *'I am the Watchful Sword of the Warrior. My brothers are my hilt. None shall trespass against us, nor those our swords protect. I will defend the defenceless, fight for those who cannot.  I am the blade of the innocent,  the eye that sees the soul.'*

Ares kicks, his muscles spasming as his life drains. The rope pulls tight, and his body hangs limp. Equinus steps forward and places two fingers on his ankle. He then turns to the others.

> *‘None shall trespass against us.’*
