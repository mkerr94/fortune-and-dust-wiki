#chapter2 #prologue

---

A song of clashing steel split the morning air. [[Marius Flint]] lifted his blade above his head to block an oncoming blow. He braced himself for impact. Time itself seemed to slow has he caught the reflection of his own eyes in the blunted steel.

> *"I must not be afraid."*

The blow came. Gritting his teeth and pushing his heels into the dirt below, Marius held firm.

> *"I will not be afraid."*

He readied himself to riposte. As he did, he noticed a shift in the stance of his attacker from the corner of his eye. A muscular hand shot out toward him. A push on his chest, and his balance was lost. Marius fell to the ground. Stinging tears welled in his eyes and his cheeks began to redden.

> *"Footwork, runt. One day, yours will get you killed."*

[[Ares de Vries]]' voice was crystalline and cold, biting as a morning frost. He gave a dismissive, scornful snicker as he began to turn away from Marius.

> *"You're welcome to try again tomo…"*

Marius scrambled to his feet. The fear was gone. Anger rose in its place. He lunged at Ares, training sword dropping from his hand. He made to barrel, shoulder-first, into the taller man's torso. He closed his eyes and yelled. Suddenly, the breath was knocked from him, as he felt Ares' fist sink into his stomach. He gasped and fell to his knees. Dazed, he saw Ares take a step toward him, blunted sword raised in his right hand. He winced and prepared for the strike.

> *"That's enough, Ares."*

Marius opened one eye. His brother, [[Telemachus ‘Mackie’ Flint|Mackie]], was at the side of the training ground. Mackie stood firm, looking at Ares with unblinking resolve, green eyes sparkling beneath his mop of reddish-brown curls. Behind him, a small crowd had gathered to observe the confrontation. Among them, Marius could see Cerlan, the blonde half-elf. He was standing next to [[Braskin Dropstone|Braskin]], the towering Goliath, who looked on with an inscrutable expression. Ares snarled at Mackie.   

> *"Last time I looked, Telemachus, it was my duty to train your brother how to use his sword. Looks like I need to teach him to control his temper as well."*

Marius flinched as Ares once again raised his hand. Mackie reached out with his own hand, holding one of his twin hand-axes, to stop the blow. He caught Ares' arm at the wrist as it fell with the handle of his axe.

> *"I think Marius' training is done for the day."*

Ares looked to Mackie with venom in his eyes. Ignoring this, Mackie looked down to face his brother.

> *"Marius, thank your instructor for his patience and come with me."*

Marius wiped the tears from his cheeks with his forearm and stood. He couldn't yet look at Mackie or Ares, so he looked at the ground.

> *"Thank you, Ares."*

Ares did not reply. Mackie returned his hand-axe to its holster. Marius felt his brother’s arm around his shoulder as he turned him away from the training ground and began to walk. Mackie leaned down subtly to speak into Marius' ear.

> *"Just keep walking. Don't turn around."*

Marius made to do as he was told, but froze as he heard that cold voice from behind him.

> *"What say you and I teach the runt a lesson now, Telemachus?"*

Mackie paused, and Marius felt his brother's body go tense.

> *"We need nothing further from you today, Ares, thank you."*

Ares began to raise his voice, making sure those who had stopped to watch the altercation could hear. Marius stiffened as he heard his instructor's footsteps approaching them from behind. Mackie's hand tightened on his shoulder and he pushed Marius to the side as Ares came close enough to stand in front of them. This close, Ares towered over Mackie. He addressed him grimly.

> *"Oh, but I think you do. The runt needs to learn quick what it means to be a soldier, or one day he's going to put all of us in danger. There is no room for weakness in war, Telemachus, each of us must be able to defend the other. What will you do when the time comes and he isn't ready?"*

Mackie met Ares' gaze.

> *"I will be ready enough for us both."*

Ares grimaced and moved to brush past Mackie toward Marius. Marius recoiled but Ares was stopped in his tracks as Mackie stretched out an arm and rested the flat of one of his hand-axes against Ares' chest. Ares smirked.

> *"Very well."*

In one fluid movement, Ares took a side stance and swung his blunted training sword in an overhead arc at Mackie's head. Mackie side-stepped deftly, pulling his other hand-axe from its holster as he did. Balanced by the weight of them in both hands, he spun with balletic grace and caught Ares' ankle between the blade and hilt of the axe in his right hand. Then, he pulled it toward him.

The taller man fell backward to the ground, tripped. Upon landing, he rolled to the side and pushed himself upright. He smiled and once again readied his stance. Again he lunged with an overhead swing of his sword. Again Mackie side-stepped the blow. This time, however, Ares shifted his momentum to ram his shoulder into Mackie's chest. Mackie doubled over, winded, and Ares used the opportunity to push down on his shoulder - forcing him to his knees.

Marius jolted forward to intervene, but was stopped by a pair of immensely strong arms. He strained his neck to look up, and saw the stoic face of Braskin, the Goliath, staring down at him and slowly shaking his head. Marius' ears were ringing. He saw Braskin mouth something to Cerlan, just to their left, who then slipped away and disappeared.

Ares held Mackie down by the shoulder, raising his own elbow as he did. Marius saw his brother struggling to regain his breath, looking vacantly at the ground. As Ares' brought his elbow down to strike his brother's head, his sword clenched in his fist above it, Marius cried out.

There was an audible intake of breath. Almost imperceptibly, Mackie shifted to the side and raised the axe in his left hand to where his head was moments before. Unable to stop the momentum of his blow, Ares brought his elbow down hard on the upheld blade, cutting into the flesh of his arm. In shock, his grip on the hilt of his sword was lost and it fell to the ground, sending up a small cloud of dust as it landed. Mackie stood, kicking the fallen sword to the side. He stretched out his arm, pointing the flat top of his hand-axe toward Ares' chest. Ares clutched his injured elbow and met his eyes.

> *"Yield, Ares. This is over."*

A wild look came over Ares' eyes. His body tensed, poised to lunge at Telemachus.

> *"Brothers! To attention at once!"*

Marius instinctively stood up straight as Braskin's arms released their grip and he did the same beside him. Ares and Mackie separated and came to attention, staring straight ahead. Between them, Marius saw that [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu]] was approaching from his quarters, with Cerlan following close behind. He stared intensely at Telemachus and Ares.

> *"Dígame, what is the meaning of this, exactly?"*

Mackie replied without emotion.

> *"Training, Captain."*

Josias raised an eyebrow and looked Mackie up and down.

> *"With sharpened axes, I see. You will cease this farce at once before I strip them from you for good. Ares, report to the medic to see to your wound. Telemachus, take your brother to the barracks, you are confined to quarters for the night."*

Ares nodded and left without a word, marching across the open yard. Josias' eyes followed him scrutinously all the way. He returned his gaze to Mackie, who gave a short bow and turned to collect Marius. He gave a nod of the head in thanks to Braskin and then gestured for Marius to come with him. Marius kept his eyes low as he followed his brother across the dusty ground and into the dark barracks building.

Inside, Mackie paced silently to their bunk and sat down on the lower of the two beds. He let out a sigh of exhaustion. Marius approached him tentatively.

> *"You did it, brother, you beat him. You beat Ares!"*

Mackie looked up with watery eyes.

> *"I beat him? I knowingly bore arms against one of my sworn brothers, Marius. And I saw you try to do the same when he put you on the ground. What were you thinking?"*

Marius opened his mouth to protest, then hung his head and sat down next to his brother.

> *"I'm sorry, Mackie, I don't know what came over me. I was so angry, I just wanted to hurt him. I wanted to show him that I wasn't afraid, that I wasn't weak."*

Marius braced himself for a scolding, but it never came. He glanced to the side at his brother, who was staring down at an object in his hands. It was his locket, the one he always carried around his neck. He clicked open the casing, and revealed the small portrait within. Penny, their sister.

Marius recalled a bed. A simple room in a simple house. A small, wire-framed body within. Pale skin and mousy hair, soaked with sweat. A cold hand to hold. He heard that cough again. The one he had listened to so many nights. Falling asleep to its rhythm. It had become like the ticking of a clock. So regular, so soft, so reliable, that you eventually don’t notice it at all. Until it stops. He remembered an empty chair and a terrible, terrible silence.

Beside him, Mackie sighed, then spoke softly. His eyes looked through the pictogram within the locket, seeing something else. Seeing a smile that only remained in the deep pools and quiet ripples of memory long held dear.

> *“Do you remember the night she died, little brother?”*

Marius paused before answering.

> *“We were there with her. We held her hand. There was nothing we could do.”*

Mackie thumbed at the locket thoughtfully.

> *“I went out, after, do you remember? Into the rain. Do you remember how it used to rain? So close to the sea. I went to the Tower of the Servant. I remember walking among the bars and taverns. There was music playing. People shouting, playing their games of dice. Squabbling, bickering, laughing, drinking. As if nothing had happened. As if the world hadn’t ended.”*

Marius thought back to that night.

> *“You said you were going to get the doctor, but you came back bruised. Your face. You were robbed on the way back.”*

Mackie winced.

> *“I lied. I went to drink. I went to not be in that room with you and Penny.”*

Marius watched as his brother hung his head.

> *“Walking among those people, Marius. It was as if every one of them was laughing at me. It made me angry. So full of rage. Someone bumped into me on the street. Or maybe I bumped into him. I threw my fist and just kept hitting. More joined, more piled in, but I just kept hitting and hitting and hitting. Eventually, they were all on the floor. I came back. You were still there, alone with Penny.*

Mackie rubbed at his temples with his thumb and forefinger.

> *" I was a sworn brother of the Protectorate, tasked with defending those who needed it. That night I failed you, I failed Penny, and I failed [[The Warrior]]. I was reported. Next day, the Commander told me I was to be posted here, and I knew I had to take you with me. It’s my fault we are here, Marius, I’m sorry. One day, I’ll pay for it, and I will deserve to do so.”*

Marius thought for a moment, then put his hand on his brother’s shoulder.

> *“Why are you telling me this, Mackie? There’s nothing to forgive. You had just lost your sister.”*

Mackie turned to look at him.

> *“I’m telling you because that same anger is in you. No good can come from it Marius, no matter how good it might feel. It’s not who you are.”*

Marius considered this, and thought back to his clash with Ares.

> *“Anger gives you strength, brother. It took away my fear.”*

Mackie sighed.

> *“You’re thinking about Ares. He’s strong, yes. But his anger makes him bitter, it makes him reckless.”*

Mackie clicked the locket shut and stood quickly, turning to wipe a tear from his eye and collecting himself with a put-on smile. He picked up one of his hand-axes and showed it to Marius.

> *“What do you see when you look at this?”*

Marius looked at the blade.

> *“It’s an axe.”*

Mackie held it up.

> *“And what is an axe?”*

Marius replied.

> *“It’s a weapon.”*

Mackie handed the axe to his brother.

> *“Wrong. It’s a tool. A weapon has only one purpose: to cause harm. It can only be used in anger. This is how Ares uses violence. A tool, on the other hand, requires no moral predisposition. It is deployed only for the task that demands it, by a skilled and steady hand. This is how you must use violence. You are a trained soldier of the Protectorate, Marius, or at least you will be. Your sword is an extension of that training. It is not given to you to cause harm, but to prevent harm from being inflicted upon others. This is how you must use it, brother. What are our words?”*

Marius recited them, as he had done so many times before.

> *“I am the Watchful Sword of the Warrior. My brothers are my hilt. None shall trespass against us, nor those our swords protect. I will defend the defenceless, fight for those who cannot.  I am the blade of the innocent,  the eye that sees the soul.”*

Mackie reached down to cup his brother’s face in his hands. His eyes met Marius’ and he smiled.

> *“Your brothers are your hilt. Remember that well, Marius. That is where you will find your strength. That is how you will forget your fear.”*
