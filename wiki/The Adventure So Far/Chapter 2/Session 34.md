# The Events of Session 34

[[Amotti]] blinks his eyes open, squinting in the high afternoon sun. As his vision returns, he sees [[Silas Seer]] standing over him, looking concerned. He looks around. The cave, [[Vel]], and the strange bioluminescent flora from before he lost consciousness are gone. All that remains is the prairie, dusty winds blowing over its sparse terrain, exactly as it was. In his confusion, Amotti begins to panic and repeat the words of the vow he took all that time ago, in another life.

> *"In Twilight's embrace, we find our place… in Twilight's embrace, we find our place…*"

Silas places a hand on Amotti's shoulder, urging him to come to his senses and calm himself. Instead, Amotti becomes more and more erratic, the red and blue auras of his twin gods shining from his earrings. As his demands to Silas to explain Vel's disappearance and to take him back to the gave become more urgent and his body language more threatening, the red begins to consume the blue and his balance is tipped. A wild look comes over Amotti and Silas takes a step back, hand on the hilt of his scimitar. However, just as things look likely to descend into violence, Amotti regains control. Breathing a sigh of relief, Silas suggests that they make their way back to Fortuna and find something for Amotti to eat, to which Amotti agrees and the two make their way back to the outpost gates.

Meanwhile, [[Sel]], [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]], [[Gambit]] and [[Pastor Jeremiah|Jeremiah]] discuss the note purporting to be from [[Telemachus ‘Mackie’ Flint|Telemachus]]. They discuss several theories. Could Telemachus really be alive? Could this note have been falsified in order to lure Ares to the armoury? Would someone in camp recognise the author's handwriting? As they talk, Jeremiah visibly becomes weaker. His breathing becomes heavier and his form appears to shimmer in the shady light of the barracks tent. Gambit suggests that he sit down, and he does so. Jeremiah places one hand on the frame of the bunk bed and, almost as if lost in a sudden mist, he disappears from view. Unsure of the meaning of this or how to react, the remaining Players decide their best course of action is to regroup with the others.

In the training yard, they meet up with [[Wild Bill]], [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]] and [[Marius Flint]]. They proceed to the captain's quarters with Marius in tow, seeking audience with [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias]]. The group are puzzled to find that the captain is gone. Wild Bill and Lythara exit the tent to look for him. They find Josias underneath the west-most perimeter stairway, at a small shrine placed in a hollow between the underside of the staircase the perimeter wall. They see that Josias is praying to a small, candlelit statuette of [[The Warrior]]. The figure is depicted in plate armour, standing tall and proud. In his hands, he holds the hilt of an upturned sword. At its centre, is carved an ever watchful eye. Josias finishes his prayer and returns to his quarters with Wild Bill and Lythara.

Inside, Josias asks how Marius is faring, placing a kind hand on his shoulder. He then sends the boy to the leftmost alcove of the tent to wait for a while and discusses what's to be done with the Players. Knowing Marius' eventual fate having witnessed his strange arrival in [[Clement's Hollow]], the Players and Josias agree that he must be watched over carefully. However, Josias urges against confining Marius to quarters, saying that this will only serve to distress him and raise questions among the others. Instead, he advises that the Players allow Marius to go about his business in camp, watching him from a distance - to which they agree.

With this decided, the conversation then turns to the progress of the Player's investigations and their suspicions as to the identity of the killer in camp. The group raise with Josias that they observed [[Braskin Dropstone|Braskin]] spit to the ground when [[Ares de Vries|Ares]]' was mentioned during Josias' morning address. The captain considers this gravely, however voices doubts about Braskin's capability. He is strong yes, and would have been physically able to overcome Ares. However, Josias stresses the planning required to stage Ares' suicide, and states that he does not think Braskin intellectually capable of concocting such a scheme. This leads the group to wonder if, assuming Braskin did murder Ares, he had help in doing so. They then ask who Braskin is closest with in camp. Josias replies that this was Telemachus but that, now Mackie is dead, Silas is the first person who comes to mind. The Players decide to split up to continue their investigation. Eli, Sel and Lythara will go to the mess hall to find Braskin and see what information they can gather from him. Wild Bill and Gambit will revisit [[Festus 'Anvil' Brim|Anvil]], hoping to gain something further from the armourer than they were able to at the first pass.

First, however, Eli reveals the note discovered in the barracks to Josias. The captain admits that he does not recognise the handwriting and reiterates that, although Mackie's body was not recovered, there is no chance that he would have been able to survive alone in the dust-dweller's cavern for two weeks. He recalls Marius to the group, and they show him the note. Reading it, Marius becomes visibly distressed. He knows that Mackie is dead, he couldn't have written this note. When it is suggested to him that the note may have been used to lure Ares to the armoury, he dismisses the thought.

> *"Ares did this to himself. No one else."*

Eli puts out his hand to retrieve the note from Marius, which he hands over reluctantly. Josias suggests that Marius stays in the captain's quarters for a moment to collect himself. The Players then leave to pursue their separate tasks, leaving Marius and Josias alone in the tent. Before they go, Josias privately urges caution on their part. The outright accusation of one of the soldiers without concrete evidence could cause chaos among the ranks if handled indiscreetly.

Concurrently, Amotti and Silas return through the outpost's main gate. Silas suggests that they visit [[Ladle]], the camp chef, in the kitchens. However, on their way they encounter [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] as he exits the nearby stables after helping Equinus with mucking out duty. Caeldrid asks for a moment alone with Amotti, and they seek privacy in the shadow of a small outbuilding near the training yard while Silas continues on to the kitchens.  

Eli, Sel and Lythara find Braskin in the mess hall, eating some stew while on break. He regards them warily as they enter. They ask Braskin about the night they arrived in camp. Specifically, about the end of he and Silas' watch over the Players in the lock-up. Braskin states that he and Silas were due to be relieved by Cerlan and Berstol for the second half of the shift, however they were instead met by Josias and told to report for duty on the perimeter wall. They then ask Braskin about his relationship with Ares and Silas. He admits that he had little regard for Ares, but describes Silas as "the smartest person he knows." All the while, the Players get the sense that Braskin is a man of few thoughts and even fewer words. Further still, as his responses to them become increasingly glib and deflective, the Players surmise that he may simply be repeating what another has told him to say.

One by one, the Players return to the central yard. Eli, Sel and Lythara exit the mess hall. Wild Bill and Gambit cross the yard on their way to the armoury. Caeldrid and Amotti continue to talk privately behind the training yard. Marius and Josias emerge from the captain's quarters. Suddenly, sounds of a commotion arise from the stables. The neighing and whinnying of a horse in distress and, soon after, the splintering of wood and the pounding of hooves. All of the Players direct their attention in this direction. On the wall and in [[Ghrist Skylark]]'s watchtower, the soldiers eyes also turn. As they do, a horse bursts from the stable doors. Frantically, it races from point to point in the yard, terrified and unsure of its next turn. In its frenzy, the horse clatters into the low fence of the training area. There is a sickening snap like the splintering of a branch as one of the creature's front legs breaks with the impact. The horse collapses to the ground. With a swift wave of his hand, Josias signals to Skylark. There is a whistle of an arrow through the air and a dull thud as it sinks into the animal's throat. The whites of its eyes fading, the horse dies with a last heave of its chest.

Josias marches forward grimly and shouts to his men.

> *"To your posts! Ahora! Now! All of you! There is nothing further to see."*

He and the Players approach the body. Josias asks them to fetch Equinus from the stables and inspect the interior, and Eli alone volunteers. While he is away, Gambit theorises to Wild Bill that this may have been arranged on purpose to cause a distraction among the soldiers. He and Bill investigate, Bill looking outside the still-open main gate and Gambit peering over the perimeter wall with the help of Ralf. Despite their efforts, both find nothing to suggest the horse's escape was orchestrated. Eli and [[Equinus Roth|Equinus]] emerge from the stables. Equinus kneels to inspect the horse, and pleads to Josias that he has no idea what happened. He explains that the horses were restless, and when he approached to calm them this one reared and bolted.

As Equinus inspects the horse, Sel uses the opportunity to ask him about Ares' death, pointing to it as another inexplicable event that has occurred in the space of just one day in camp. Equinus becomes frustrated, insisting that Sel lets him continue with his work and dismissing Ares' death as unrelated. Sel instead turns to Josias, and asks after Telemachus' locket, which the captain reluctantly gives him. Sel insists the locket is key to his continued investigations.

As the soldiers return to their posts and Josias orders Equinus to dispose of the horse's remains, Wild Bill and Gambit continue with their original intention to visit Anvil in the armoury. Much as before, they find him hammering away at a piece of softened leather. This time it is much smaller, and they recognise that it must be intended for Gambit. Approaching the armourer, Wild Bill startles him, and Anvil bemoans the surprise.

> *"Tween you and all this commotion, I really ought to start locking them doors I reckon."*

They confirm with Anvil that this is where Ares' body was found, and that he was the first to discover it. Gambit asks if there was anything unusual about it, and Anvil replies.

> *"Now I come to think of it, yeah. I seem to remember there was a ruddy, great rope tied round his neck."*

Anvil continues that he found Ares' body hanging from the steel rafters of the armoury. The rope had been secured to one of the pillars around the edges of the room, and swung over one of the rafters to be secured in place. With Ralf's help, Gambit inspects the rafters and does indeed find rope fibres there. Wild Bill asks how Ares might have hanged himself from this height, and Anvil reveals a wooden stool that was found underneath him. Ares must have stood on this and then kicked it away to remove his support. Gambit notes however that, despite the floor of the armoury being covered in soot and the soles of their shoes being  dirtied accordingly, the top of the stool is completely unmarked. This strikes him as stranger still given that Anvil himself is also covered in soot and doesn't appear to routinely keep his person or workstations clean. He and Wild Bill thank Anvil, and make their departure. Outside the armoury, Gambit reveals to Wild Bill that he now suspects Anvil to be Ares' killer, and the two discuss what to do next.

Caeldrid and Amotti discuss Amotti’s vision out on the prairie, and Amotti explains its connection to his troubled past. He tells Caeldrid that he and his sister are part of a powerful Drow family. From birth, he and [[Maeve]] were groomed into the service of the Spider Goddess, [[Lolth]]. While Maeve became a devoted follower and servant of the Flesh-Carver, Amotti resisted the darker aspects of the faith and took on also the philosophies of [[Eilistraee]], Drow goddess of beauty and moonlight. Caught between two opposing divine forces, Amotti’s inner conflict drove him into the care of a Drow sect known as [[the Twilight Keepers]]. This group sought to achieve a balance between the light and dark, sacred Twilight, drawing on the power of both Lolth and Eilistraee. Amotti found solace in their teachings. Vel was their leader, and Amotti’s mentor. His surrogate father. Through his mentorship, Amotti learned to harness the power of both goddesses and strive always for the balance within his own soul.

> *“In Twilight’s embrace, we find our place. Thriving in shadows, striving in light’s embrace. For harmony’s sake, both light and dark we bear. Ensuring the balance with sacrificial care. For the balance.”*

During this time, Amotti and Maeve’s house (house Xai) became the centre of a conflict with a neighbouring settlement of surface dwellers. Maeve led their armies, slaughtering man, woman and child as she saw fit. Amotti could feel the balance tipping in favour of darkness. In his desperation, he prayed to Lolth. Lolth offered her power, in exchange for a terrible price. Blood demands blood, and Amotti was tasked with an unforgivable sacrifice. Seeing no other choice, he sought out and killed Vel, his mentor. Heartbroken and dejected, he returned to his sister and confessed his actions.

Maeve seethed with rage, but not for the loss of Vel. She told Amotti of how a dark mist had swept the battlefield, wiping out house Xai’s forces and sparing the surface dwellers. In her fury, she attacked Amotti and chased him from the Underdark, branding him a traitor and swearing on their shared blood to hunt him down as far as he ran and kill him for what he had done. This is why Amotti fled to Alcadeña.

Caeldrid takes all of this in. He admits to Amotti that the weight of the situation is beyond his understanding, and that he can offer no more assistance than he has already given. He encourages Amotti to tell the others, which Amotti thinks on carefully.

“Yes, perhaps the burden can be shared…”
