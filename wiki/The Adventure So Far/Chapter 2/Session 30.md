# The Events of Session 30

The Players continue their struggle against [[The Chronovores]]. [[Wild Bill]] and [[Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] push on through the dust-storm, following Ralf, and rejoin [[Sel]] and [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] on the other side. This leaves [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]], [[Gambit]] and [[Amotti]] to fend off the creatures alone. Amotti is best on all sides, as he suffers attack after attack, being thrown about in the air as the chronovores attempt to make off with him in their grasp. He is gripped in one of the creature’s beaks, but is released after Gambit distracts it with a shot from his revolver. Crashing to the ground and injured to the point of unconsciousness, Amotti is saved by Lythara – who despite her better judgement charges to his rescue. Together, she, Amotti and Gambit finally burst through the dust-storm and join their companions in the stillness on the other side.  

The Players find themselves in a wide, open plain, seeking the [[Fortuna Outpost]]. The landscape here has patches of dry grass and sparse, withering foliage. Behind them, there is no sign of the maelstrom or the creatures that they have just escaped. Eli searches for any recognizable landmark behind them, trying to discern if they have somehow been transported somewhere else entirely. In doing so, the Players collectively realise that Rusty Jack, Pistachio, MB and the wagons are still tied up on the other side of the storm. With nothing to be done about this, the Players discuss the ordeal they have just come through, and in particular give theories as to the chronovores strange feeding behaviour near the storm and why they initially seemed to only target Sel. Sel hesitantly tests walking a short distance back in the direction they came from, but finds nothing other than a strange, unsettling sensation in his stomach. Caeldrid attempts to commune with Gwynevere and finds that he cannot, leading him and Amotti to briefly discuss Gwynevere’s mysterious origins. Caeldrid explained that she simply appeared to him a few months ago.  

The group push on to the West, with the late-afternoon sun beginning to set in front of them. They eventually reach a fort, constructed of fortified wood and iron brackets. From around a quarter-mile out from its gates, they hear the sound of a horn ringing clear in the fading light. Gambit spots two flags hanging at an angle from poles either side of the front gate. They bear a joint insignia – the Watchful Sword of [[The Protectorate]] and the six-spoked ship’s wheel of [[Alcadeña]]. Given the state of the soldier who arrived in [[Clement's Hollow]], the Players are surprised to find the outpost in seemingly good condition. As they approach, they are greeted by two guards standing on the perimeter wall above the gate. One is a graceful half-elven man with cropped blonde hair. He bears a spear tied with a yellow scarf below the blade and observes the players with a confidence bordering on pomp. The other is a human male of olive skin and dark eyes. His head is shaved and his leather armour lacks greaves or pauldrons – leaving his bare arms exposed. He surveys the Players intently and in silence.  

The Players are greeted and told that the captain of the outpost, [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias Bartolomeu]], has been informed of their arrival. They are to be brought to him for audience provided they surrender their weapons. Reluctantly, the group agree, however Eli conceals a small dagger beneath his cloak and Gambit manages to stash his revolver wrapped up in his cloak inside Ralf’s chest cavity. Sel does not offer his Staff of Water Binding, and the watchmen do not request it from him. The olive-skinned human shouts down to an unseen colleague to open the gate, and the Players hear the scraping of wood as it swings open. They see that the gateway leads into an open central area, and two more Protectorate soldiers stand in the opening. One is a lilac-skinned tiefling with red eyes. He carries a scimitar at his hip. The other is a towering goliath of pale grey-blue skin with dark tribal markings. On his back he carries a double-bladed battle axe (see [[Fortuna Soldiers]]). The goliath collects their weapons and they are lead through into the fort.  

The Players are escorted through the central training area of the outpost. They notice that the site consists of several sub-structures built within the walls. On their way to the Captain’s Quarters, they notice other soldiers on duty who note their passing with varying levels of curiosity. They see three soldiers training in the sword using wooden dummies. One of the soldiers is reptilian in nature, with a sinewy body covered in bright green scales and a phosphorescent orange frill which expands and contracts vertically above his head and below his chin. The training is led by a tall, muscular human of thick shoulders and braided white hair. He regards the Players coldly with piercing blue-grey eyes from beneath a stern brow. Looking around carefully, Caeldrid feels that something is off about the outpost. Eventually, he pinpoints his doubt – the facility appears to be drastically under-manned for its size. The Players are led past a simple stone well to the left of the training area. For the briefest moment, Eli catches a glimpse of what he thinks is a small girl running past the well and laughing. She has curly golden locks which tumble to her shoulders. 

The party reach the Captain’s Quarters. They are warned by their escort that mage-craft is strictly prohibited on the outpost grounds, as it is in all of Alcadeña without license. The interior is richly decorated, and the Player’s see a cushioned bedchamber, some ceremonial armour and a small candlelit shrine in the antechambers off of the main room. In the centre, an ornate wooden war-table stands on a large rug. On it, is spread a huge map that is littered with various wooden figurines. Hunched over this map, studying it intently with intense, dark-brown eyes, is Captain Josias Bartolomeu. Josias looks up at the group, gestures for the escort to leave them for the time being, and greets the Player’s in a tongue flecked with the Alcadeñan parlance. 

> *“Venga, por favor. Welcome to Fortuna. Please, state your business.”* 

The Players tell Josias that they have come from Clement’s Hollow, and recall the letter sent by Old Mags’ requesting aid from Josias and his men. The captain regards them with intense curiosity. He asks after the well-being of the town, to which the Players respond that it has survived and is rebuilding. This sparks further consternation, as Josias produces Mags’ letter and says that he received it just this morning. This prompts Sel to ask the Captain what day it is, to which he responds that it is the 27th Eleint (by the Player’s reckoning it is, in fact, the 31st). Sel points out that this can only be due to some distortion in time itself, and Josias sighs. 

> *“It is as I feared then, they do say that sickness confuses the mind…”* 

Josias beckons to his soldiers, who quietly enter the room from behind the Players bearing shackles. 

> *“Please, forgive me friends. This is for your own good. Magnolia’s letter spoke of a sickness rampaging through Clement’s Hollow – and we cannot be sure that you are clear of it. You must be quarantined before we can. I promise you, you will be released when we are certain.”* 

The Players protest. As he is shackled, Wild Bill attempts to headbutt the goliath soldier, who shrugs off the blow and restrains him. Amotti attempts to use his mage-hand to steal Mags’ letter from Josias, but as he is shackled feels the warmth of his magical ability drain from him and the letter drops to the floor. In a last attempt to persuade the captain from his course of action, Sel produces [[The Soldier's Locket]]. Josias takes it slowly and looks on with a grave mixture of fear and confusion. He takes a step back and studies it with one hand, using the other to instruct the soldiers restraining the Players to take them away. Almost to no-one, he mutters. 

> *“Where did you get this…”* 

The Players are escorted across the yard into a lock-up, and into a cell lined with benches big enough to hold them all. As the lilac-skinned tiefling guard locks them in he looks at the Players with ruby eyes. 

> *“Captain’s orders…”*
