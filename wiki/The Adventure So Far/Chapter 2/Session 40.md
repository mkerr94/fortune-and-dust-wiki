# The Events of Session 40

[[Silas Seer|Silas]] is pushed from the gallows, with the noose tightening around his neck. [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]], waiting below, manages to catch him at the moment he falls. Upon making physical contact with Silas, Caeldrid receives two visions:

*You are in a cell in an island prison. Pale grey sunlight filters into the room, and you hear the sound of waves crashing against the rocks below the barred window. You huddle your small Tiefling tail to your chest and shiver as a pounding starts at the door.*

*The vision flickers and you see a tall, muscular human figure in Protectorate guard uniform whipping a bound, bear of a man. The guard turns and hands the whip to you. When you refuse, he lifts his hand to strike you.*

Meanwhile, [[Sel]] successfully casts a minor illusion to mimic the sound of Silas' neck snapping at the moment the rope pulls tight. [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] surveys the gathered crowd. There is no screaming, no riot. Just the gentle prairie wind blowing over the ground. Beside [[Wild Bill]], young [[Marius Flint]] looks on with tears in his eyes but a renewed air of resolve. On the gallows, [[Equinus Roth|Equinus]] steps forward to check Silas pulse by placing two fingers on his neck. He turns to [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias]] and gives a solemn shake of the head. Josias draws his sword, cuts the rope from above Silas in one swift strike and the body of the Tiefling plunges into the hole below.

Caeldrid is pulled from his vision. Before he can check Silas for signs of life, the space beneath the gallows is illuminated as the rear door is opened. He places Silas on the ground and backs into a dark corner. Equinus steps through the open door. He crouches to move into the space beneath the gallows, and looks from side to side before collecting Silas in his arms. He carries him through the opening and Caeldrid swiftly follows, still obscured by Sel's spell of invisibility.

In the yard, Josias dismisses the gathered Soldiers, and the Players see him follow Equinus as he carries Silas' body into his quarters. The Players retreat to the barracks and Caeldrid is made visible again, they frantically discuss their next move. Fearing their ploy may be discovered with Silas having been taken to the Captain's Quarters, they decide to go and confront Josias. Eli proposes that they tell the Captain that Silas requested a sea burial in the harbour of Portumluna and offer to escort his body there. They leave [[Gambit]] and [[Amotti]] behind in the barracks.

However, when they enter Josias' tent, the captain is already in conversation with an expressionless Equinus. He tells the Players that Silas is to be buried together with Ares and Braskin, at a spot outside of camp. Eli protests and states that Silas has the right to be buried near his kin, but Josias rebuffs him:

> *"You misunderstand, he has forfeited that right. Yet he will still be buried with his brothers."*

Outside, three bells toll in slow succession. Josias turns to Equinus and nods, and the two leave the tent. Sel uses the opportunity to briefly place his hand on Silas' body, looking for signs of life. To his initial shock, he can feel nothing. However, after a few minutes, he feels a slight warmth from the Tiefling's skin and the faintest heartbeat from within his chest.

Meanwhile, Amotti begins to scour the barracks for useful items, raiding the soldier's storage chests. He finds little of value, save some coin, but does decide to keep some personal letters he discovers. One, clearly belonging to [[Ladle]], details a recipe for owl-buffalo stew. Another is written in a language neither he nor Gambit can recognise. This is accompanied by a charcoal drawing of a beautiful woman with piercing dark eyes and flowing hair. Finally, he finds what appears to be a notice of release from [[Orsobia]] prison.

The others leave Josias' tent, and find the soldiers gathered in two lines, awaiting the procession of the dead. The bodies of [[Ares de Vries|Ares]], [[Braskin Dropstone|Braskin]] and Silas are retrieved and placed on wooden carts. Josias then leads a procession through the open gate into the prairie. The Players follow, and see that Cerlan and [[Ghrist Skylark]] stay behind to watch the perimeter wall.

The procession marches on in silence for almost half a mile to the East. Eventually, the Players see the outline of a tree on the horizon, next to a large boulder. Approaching, they see Berstol standing with a shovel before three freshly dug graves and three wooden coffins. However, their attention is instantly drawn to a wagon and three horses pitched to the boulder beside the tree. The Players recognise the wagon as their own and the horses as Rusty Jack, Pistachio and MB. Wild Bill sees to their feeding and watering and greets Rusty Jack gratefully.

The prairie wind brushes through the dry grass. The horses snort and tap their hooves. Above, the thin and brittle branches of the burial tree creak softly. It stands in judgment of those gathered, ready to receive the dead to the earth beneath. Josias begins the burial proceedings.

> *"We now commit this flesh to the earth, free of the terrible consequence of life. May the Warrior judge their souls fit to be accepted once more into his ranks. If the judgement is failed, may their bodies serve to nourish the land from which they have taken nourishment. That which once breathed the sweet air may do so no longer. They go now to a fate of Fortune and Dust."*

With this, the three bodies are put in their respective coffins. Anvil nails the lids shut, and they are lowered to the ground. Equinus looks nervously at Eli. Josias picks up the shovel, and places one mound of dirt on each of the coffins in turn. He then leaves and slowly walks back to camp, his head bowed. Each of the gathered brothers do the same in a ceremonial fashion, eventually leaving just the Players with Berstol and Equinus.

Berstol begins to fill in the graves in earnest, but Sel intervenes. He charms Berstol with magic.

> *"I… think you… should take a load off. It is… a beautiful… day…"*

A vacant look comes over Berstol, and he places the shovel on the ground. He walks behind the burial tree and sits, looking distantly out onto the endless stretch of prairie. Amotti casts a spell of silence around him, and the Players regroup with Equinus in their newfound privacy. He is frantic.

> *"Listen, I managed to sedate Silas in the Captain's tent, but what the fck are we going to do now? He'll wake up soon and he's four feet in the earth inside a fcking box."*

Ralf lowers Gambit into Silas' grave, and he gets to work unfastening the coffin lid. Amotti then raises Silas' body out onto the level ground and they place him on their wagon, still unconscious. Gambit instructs Ralf to backfill the graves and they use the opportunity to take a short rest.

Just as the rhythmic sound of the shovel scraping on dirt threatens to become almost peaceful, they are shocked as Silas takes a sharp intake of breath and sits upright in the cart. Almost instantly, he falls back against the wood. Then, unceremoniously, he pushes himself over the side of the cart and vomits onto the ground. Equinus checks him over, and Silas thanks the group for saving him.

Knowing they cannot return to camp, Silas realises he must now leave and live as an outlaw. The group discuss where to go next, and Sel suggests [[Underton]] mining town. Initially hesitant given the risk of meeting the remainder of the Protectorate force sent there by Josias, Silas reconsiders.

> *"Wait… wait. That could work! If we can get to Underton, we could take the train along [[the Coal Track]]. We could ride it all the way to Portumluna. From there I could make passage back to Faerun. We must be careful though, I can't be seen. Most of our brothers were sent there to investigate why the coal shipments have stopped."*

Caeldrid reminds the group of the strange correspondence from Underton shown to them by Hermus, and the Players consider that they are unsure of what they might find when they reach the mine. They decide to push on regardless, given precious few alternatives. Journey to Portumluna by horse would take weeks, Clement's Hollow lies beyond the Dust-dweller's canyon and they still have not ascertained the whereabouts of Eva or the Marismentan militia.

Equinus tells the group that Underton is around two day's ride away to the northwest, and that there is an old military trial behind the outpost that they can follow to get there.  Their next destination decided, the Players give Equinus and Silas a moment to make their goodbyes. Equinus turns to Eli.

> *"Thank you, brother. I wish you good fortunes in the wars to come."*

The Players set out for Underton with Silas. Silas explains that, before the Protectorate force was sent to Underton, Josias was tracking a group of [[The Rogue Marismente|Rogue Marismente]]. They were intercepted outside of Fortuna, and one of them was captured. This individual has since been sent on to [[Samosetia]], the capital, for questioning. Silas believes there was suspicion in the Captain’s mind that the Marismentan militia could have been behind the faltering of coal supplies from Underton and that this bolstered his decision to send soldiers there.

After travelling northwest for a while, giving the outpost a wide berth, the Players come to what Silas confirms as the start of the military trail leading to the mine. Here, the prairie grass grows taller, reaching the bellies of the horses, and the trail snakes its way off into the distance. Amotti copies one word from the letter in the unknown language and shows it to Silas. He recognises it as "esposo", meaning "husband" in the Alcadeñan dialect.

Meanwhile, in the Fortuna outpost barracks, the remaining Protectorate brothers gather by candlelight. Equinus raises a glass and raises a toast.

> *"To Silas…"*
