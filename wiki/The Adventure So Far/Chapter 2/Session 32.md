# The Events of Session 32

The Players await their inspection by [[Equinus Roth]] in [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Captain Josias]]' quarters. Josias leaves to fetch Equinus, and [[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]] uses the opportunity to investigate his quarters. She finds only a few administrative papers, some coins and various earrings but declines to take anything. Meanwhile, [[Sel]] and the others inspect the large map of [[Alcadeña]] on Josias' war table. For the first time, they see the locations of the region's major settlements and geographical landmarks. Using a quill and some cloth, [[Wild Bill]] manages to make a crude sketch of the relative positions of some of the major points of interest noted on the map.

Josias returns with Equinus. The Players see that he is a tall, burly Firbolg with reddish-brown fur and rough hands. One by one, he inspects the Players' eyes, tongues and a drop of their blood from a pinprick. Satisfied that they are clear of infection, Equinus is then asked by Josias to gather the men in the training yard for an announcement. After a few moments, the Players follow the men outside and find the rest of the garrison awaiting them. Josias makes his announcement.

> *"As you all know by now, we received visitors from [[Clement's Hollow]] yesterday evening. As a precaution, they were held in the lock-up for safety overnight. Today, I am releasing them and returning their weapons.*
> 
> *This group come to us from the Rail Company, and they have business in the area. However, while our numbers are depleted, they have agreed to bolster our ranks as part of their employment – until our forces return. Please, welcome them to your ranks and be grateful of the extra sword at your side. They will be needed in the days to come.*
> 
> *I would also like to address this morning's events. [[Ares de Vries]] was our brother. He was one of us. That we did not see the demons that plagued him is a shame that I will carry forever. He will be given a funeral fitting for a soldier of the Protectorate in time, but until then, mourn in whichever way you deem fit – but we remain at our posts. As you were."*

During this eulogy, [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] and [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] inspect the gathered crowd to assess their body language and reactions. At the mention of Ares' name, Eli sees [[Braskin Dropstone|Braskin]] clear his mouth of spittle, however cannot confidently dismiss this as coincidence or simple apathy.

With the announcement made and the other soldiers returning to their posts, Josias advises the Players to integrate themselves in the camp and introduce themselves to their new comrades. Later they will be assigned specific duties on rotation. Left at last to their own devices, the group decide to split up.

[[Gambit]] and Wild Bill make their way to the armoury, the place where Ares' body was discovered earlier in the morning, to make initial enquires with the armourer, [[Festus 'Anvil' Brim]], known to the soldiers as Anvil. Pushing through the open door, they find the interior loud and hot, with a large contraption pulling hot water through a furnace making enough sound that they cannot be heard over it when standing near the door. They approach Anvil, who sits on a simple wooden stool and hammers away at treated leather on a mannequin. Anvil is a squat, stocky dwarf with frizzy black-grey hair. As he turns to Gambit and Wild Bill, he lifts a metal face shield to look at them, revealing that his right eye is missing, leaving only an empty scorched and black socket.  He also removes a pair of large blacksmith's gloves, although his hands underneath remain covered in soot.

Gambit and Wild Bill converse with Anvil. They ask to be outfitted with arms and armour, to which Anvil replies that he can provide them with something suitable if available, but they will have to return it upon leaving the outpost. Resources are tight, and he only has so much he can spare. He provides Wild Bill with a leather chest plate, from which he chisels out the [[The Protectorate|Protectorate]] sigil, but says that Gambit will have to return later for something his size. The pair ask for guns, which Anvil says he can provide, but that it is not his area of expertise but rather Captain Josias'.

The conversation then turns to Equinus. Gambit tells Anvil that they have been cleared by the doctor, to which Anvil laughs heartily. He explains that Equinus is not the doctor, merely the stablemaster and veterinarian, and is acting as de facto medic while the real camp medic is off with the bulk of the outpost's forces elsewhere. Wild Bill then ventures to ask about Ares, and how close Anvil was with him. Anvil tells them that he and Ares were no closer than Ares was with any of the other soldiers. He muses that you can never truly tell what demons lie within.

> *"Everyone has 'em, I s'ppose. Trick is not to let 'em catch up with you."*

He also reiterates to Gambit and Wild Bill that Ares was not well-liked around camp, with a notorious short-fuse and stand-offish personality. Gambit and Wild Bill leave the armoury, unsure if they have learned anything of real value to their investigation.

Meanwhile, Amotti and Sel take it upon themselves to investigate the temporal anomaly the Players have detected since arriving at Fortuna. Amotti insists that the first and best course of action would be to leave the outpost and try to travel as far away as they can. Sel agrees to help him in this. Unable to easily lift the bar on the main gate, they ask for the assistance of [[Silas Seer]], who is training nearby with Berstol and Uster. At first puzzled by their desire to leave, he tells them that they will need the approval of Josias to exit the gate. Sel leaves to seek it in the Captain's Quarters.

In Sel's absence, Amotti tries to climb the outer wall at the main gate. He makes a wager with Silas, stipulating that, should he succeed, Silas owes him a favour. Silas agrees, and outlines his own condition with a smile.

> *"Should you fail, I'd like you to make a proper introduction for me to that friend of yours."*

Amotti, misunderstanding Silas' desire to get closer to Lythara, turns to look at the departing Sel and shrugs a bemused 'ok'.

Amotti tries, and fails, to scale the main gate. However, during his attempt, Josias and Sel call Silas back across the training yard and the Captain instructs Silas to accompany the pair out of the gate for a brief patrol. On Silas and Sel's return, Amotti tries to convince them that he managed to make the climb, but Silas shrugs this off and dismisses him. Braskin the Goliath opens the gate for the trio, narrowing his eyes as he watches them pass.

Lythara, Caeldrid and Eli decide to join the watch on the perimeter wall, which they see is currently being manned by Berstol and Braskin. They make their way to the watchtower in the southwest corner of the outpost, with the intent of speaking to [[Ghrist Skylark]], the Aaracokra marksman. They find him perched in the enclosed lookout shelter at the top, and he acknowledges them without taking his sharp eyes off the horizon. He engages Lythara, Caeldrid and Eli, but appears to choose his words carefully and speaks with a reserved detachedness.

The trio discuss the death of Mackie with Skylark. Skylark laments that Mackie was the best of the soldiers at Fortuna, and a sore loss to the Protectorate. Caeldrid describes their discovery of his body and Skylark curses the Dust-dwellers for monsters. He describes his recollection of the raid with a grim expression. He explains that, on the raid of the Dust-dweller's cavern, Ares and Mackie were separated from the rest of the group. Only Ares returned, claiming that he had become trapped and called for Mackie's aid but that they were overwhelmed by their enemies. Skylark shakes his head.

> *"All I'll say is this. Mackie was Ares' sworn brother. If it were me, I'd have died before leaving that hell-hole without him."*

Lythara tells Skylark that she is adept with the bow herself, and asks to join him in the tower on watch duty. He agrees, on the condition that she clear it with the Captain first. Eli and Caeldrid decide to head back, but Skylark stops them before they do.

> *"If you're heading back below, do me a favour will you? Check on the boy. Losing his brother was hard on him, you know? Sharp lad, but brittle."*

Amotti, Sel and Silas step through the main gate, and once more feel the dust-winds pick up around them. They crunch the grit in their teeth as they step out onto the plain once more.
