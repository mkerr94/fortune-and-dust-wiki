# The Events of Session 35

[[Lythara Sunstrider|Lythara]], [[Sel]] and [[Elias Blackwood|Eli]] stand over the body of escaped horse with Captain [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias]] and [[Equinus Roth|Equinus]]. [[Captain Josias Bartolomeu|Josias]] calls over [[Braskin Dropstone]] and instructs him and Equinus to drag the body a safe distance away from the outpost and bury it. Before they do, Lythara pulls out the arrow used by [[Ghrist Skylark]] to kill the beast and inspects it, looking for signs of [[Dimeritium]] alloy, but finds the arrowhead is made of standard steel. Josias then leaves, stating that he intends to visit [[Hermus Aspall]] in the camp dovecot to attempt to send a request for aid to the bulk of their men at [[Underton]]. Lythara, Sel and Eli stand over the body of the dead horse with Captain Josias and Equinus.

As Braskin and Equinus drag away the horse, the group are rejoined by [[Wild Bill]], [[Gambit]], [[wiki/People & Organisations/Player Characters/Caeldrid Àrmann an Gealach|Caeldrid]] and [[Amotti]]. Gambit begins to voice his suspicions about [[Festus 'Anvil' Brim|Anvil]], but the Players retreat to the vacant captain's quarters to discuss in a more private setting. Despite Gambit's accusation of Anvil, Sel is still convinced that [[Silas Seer]] knows more than he has previously let on to the Players. Caeldrid also states that they are yet to talk to Hermus Aspall. As the group discuss next steps, Josias returns looking concerned. He expresses that he sensed something off about Hermus, and this confirms Caeldrid's desire to question him. The group decide to split up and continue their investigations. Caeldrid and Gambit will go to the dovecot to question Hermus. Sel has a plan to tease more information from Silas, and Eli and Lythara agree to help. Amotti and Wild Bill will go to the stables to see if any information can be gathered from the site of the horse's escape.

Caeldrid and Gambit cross the training yard, where they see young [[Marius Flint]] training with Cerlan, and ascend the steps of the dovecot tower alone. At the top, they find Hermus Aspall. Hermus is a thin, sinewy man in his late fifties. He wears thin-framed spectacles, the supports of which are pushed back into his wiry grey hair. Caeldrid and Gambit find him in a state of agitation, poring over a collection of letters spread out on his desk and muttering to himself. He is so engrossed in this activity that he doesn't immediately acknowledge their presence. In the other half of Hermus' tower room, there is a large cage containing several restless creatures. Gambit approaches to see that the [[chiropterrings]] are small, spherical winged creatures. Their bodies are covered in short, stiff black fur. They have no visible eyes or ears, but have a large mouth which, when opened, spans most of their body and reveals two rows of razor sharp teeth. They flit restlessly from perch to perch and gnaw at their cage. Hermus approaches with a stick and rattles it against the wire mesh, complaining about the noise the creatures are making.

Hermus then asks Caeldrid and Gambit their business. They state that they are just introducing themselves and Hermus eyes them shrewdly.

> *"I read that letter from [[Magnolia 'Old Mags' Aurix|Old Mags]] out at [[Clement's Hollow]] you know. She was requesting aid, not sending it. Josias may be able to fool the rest but not me, you're not from the Rail Company. Why are you here?"*

Caeldrid explains that the group are, in fact, here at the behest of the Rail Company but doesn't go as far to reveal their true purpose. He tells Hermus that, for now, they are here merely to help however they can. Hermus relents, and says that if they want to help they can help him decipher some strange correspondence that arrived from Underton just before communication from the mining colony there ceased. He shows Caeldrid and Gambit three letters, which they read.

> *"18th Eleint: First shift reported a minor seam-facing collapse in the low level tunnels. Mining operations were halted accordingly to allow a survey to be carried out. Production will decrease as a result."*

> *"20th Eleint: Survey Corps report indicates that site of collapse has serendipitously led to the discovery of previously untapped coal seams at the frontier extraction sites. Production has been cleared to continue and output should increase to record levels if new veins prove fruitful."*

> *"22nd Eleint: Expansion of newly-tapped seams has uncovered pockets of space within mine that appear to be as a result of a previous, non-authorised excavation. Relics have been found which appear to be Marismentan in origin. Samples have been returned along Coal Track for confirmation and study at Founder's University. Early evidence indicates sites may be of some religious significance. Requesting confirmation that archaeological work may not impede coal extraction."*

Hermus explains that this was the last formal correspondence received from Underton, five days ago, and that Josias then sent the bulk of his men out to investigate. Stranger still, Hermus states that confirmation was given to the miners to proceed with coal extraction, however no direct reply was received. Instead, a fourth letter, undated and written in scrawled and scratchy script, was received. Hermus shows Caeldrid and Gambit this message, which simply reads:

> *"WE ARE NOT WHO WE ARE."*

Hermus then again becomes distracted by the restless chiropterrings, and Caeldrid and Gambit use the opportunity to take their leave. They ponder on the strange messages from Underton and the seeming disconnect between the soldier's description of Hermus as a devoted carer of the messenger creatures and his cruel treatment of them during their visit.

Sel, Lythara and Eli discuss Sel's plan to extract more information from Silas regarding [[Ares de Vries|Ares]]' death. Sel reveals [[Telemachus ‘Mackie’ Flint|Telemachus]]' locket, and says that he intends to turn Eli invisible temporarily and have him plant it on Silas' person. Sel will then use this to convince Silas that Telemachus' ghost is behind Ares' murder, hoping to spook the tiefling into confessing more details than he has previously been willing to offer. Knowing that Silas has previously expressed a romantic attraction to Lythara, Sel asks her to go to the kitchens and offer to share a meal with Silas as a means of luring him out and distracting him. With Lythara heading to the kitchens, Sel and Eli find a spot hidden from view alone to perform the invisibility spell.

Lythara enters the kitchens, and finds not only Silas but [[Morris 'Ladle' Jupp]], the camp chef. Ladle is a rotund halfling, with a protruding belly and a scraggly beard. His hair is tied behind his ears underneath a bandana. When Lythara enters, Ladle starts sweeping the kitchen floor and loudly complaining about Uster having slept there the previous night and shed some scales. He explains that the lizard-folk soldier usually sleeps in the kitchens rather than the barracks as he prefers the heat of the ovens at night. Silas visibly perks up at seeing Lythara.

> *"Come come, Morris, where are your manners? Can't you see that we have a lady as our guest? Surely even you must have something fit for such a beauty.*"

Lythara, ignoring Silas' chauvinistic attempts at flirtation, asks if he would like to share a bowl of Ladle's stew with her in the mess hall. Silas enthusiastically agrees, shushing Ladle when he complains that he has only just given Lythara some stew when she was in the kitchen earlier. Lythara and Silas head outside, where they are met and interrupted by Sel. Despite showing annoyance at being parted from his lunch date, Silas agrees to Sel's request to have a private word with him and Lythara proceeds into the mess hall. Unbeknownst to the tiefling, Eli has already been turned invisible and is poised with the locket. First, however, he checks in on Lythara, alone in the mess hall and awaiting the next move.

Sel and Silas begin to talk, and Eli uses the opportunity to slip the locket into Silas' pocket. Sel panics briefly as Silas' eyes settle directly on the invisible Eli returning to the mess hall, but breathes a sigh of relief when the tiefling shakes his head and returns his attention to the conversation. Sel asks Silas if he has seen anything strange around the camp recently, explaining that some of the group have seen visions of figures from their pasts.

As he does so, he briefly touches hands with Silas and recoils as if struck by a static shock. An image flashes before his eyes. Marius Flint sits in the corner of a room, marking his arm with tally marks in charcoal and weeping. He recovers, and Silas asks if he is alright. Silas looks into Sel's eyes carefully, and for a moment a look of confusion spreads over his face. He dismisses this, and in response to Sel's question recounts that Amotti was talking about having experienced a vision when they were outside on the plains. He also tells Sel that he has had visions ever since he was a child, having been gifted with the Sight.

Sel goes a step further, asking Silas if he thinks these visions of the past can interact with present reality, if ghosts and shades could be behind the strange events in camp. Silas dismisses this off-hand, but then feels the locket against his thigh and pulls it out before him. A look of fear flashes in his eyes.

> *"That's… that's not possible. Sel, how is this here? What have you seen? What do you know?"*

Sel tells Silas about the note they found purporting to be from Telemachus, and Silas' fear grows deeper. He begins to shake and become agitated, and states that he needs to go and find Braskin. Before he leaves, Sel grabs his arm and attempts to charm him unsuccessfully.

> *"Unless you plan to hold me here against my will, Sel, I don't have to tell you anything. Now let me go."*

Silas returns the locket to Sel and walks off across the yard, looking worriedly back over his shoulder. Sel regroups with Lythara and Eli and confirms his suspicions that Silas knows something of great importance relating to the events in camp - they just have to get it out of him.

Meanwhile, Amotti and Wild Bill head to the stables alone. They find no one else inside, and Wild Bill goes to inspect one of the horses. He experiences a strange sensation. He sees the animal's vasculature lit up, as if glowing, and hears the rhythmic pulsing of its heart in his ears. He becomes aware of the heat of its blood, the saltiness and fattiness of it. He sees a figure in a shadowy corner of the stable, with long, slick black hair and an elegant, black coat. The figure smiles at Bill sinisterly, but when Bill blinks his eyes he sees only a stool and broom where the figure previously stood.

Wild Bill turns to tell Amotti what he saw and takes a step back. Amotti is changed. His eyes glow a fierce purple and purple energy rises like steam from his hands. He walks toward Wild Bill with hungry intent. Bill hesitantly raises his pistol at Amotti.

> *"If you take one more step, feller, I'll shoot - I ain't fooling around."*

Amotti continues to walk toward Wild Bill, and Bill hears a strange voice in his head. The tone is soft, female, seductive. It calls to Wild Bill and he feels a pull toward the purple light emanating from Amotti.

> *"I know you, William. I see you, inside and out. Aren't you tired of this fear? Tired of this endless struggle to outrun what you've done, what you've seen? Come to me, I'm here… I offer you the sweetest, warmest oblivion… surrender yourself to it now… leave all of this behind… why fight any longer, when you could sleep now?"*

Bill lowers his gun and sprints past Amotti, ramming him shoulder to shoulder on his way past. He emerges from the stables, looking to find help from the others. Instead, he finds that the skies outside have darkened. Standing in the centre of the training yard in a line, he sees his companions. Lythara, Sel, Gambit, Amotti, Caeldrid, Eli. Each of them has glowing purple eyes, and purple energy rising from their hands. One by one, in turn, they have all been overcome by a strange infection - a corrupting force beyond their understanding. Amotti was trying to pass this to Bill.

Bill looks around in fear, and sees the some of the soldiers of Fortuna staring down at him from the outpost walls with the same glowing purple eyes. He turns to flee from the main gate, but sees Braskin dropping the large post into place and locking it shut. He draws his double-sided battle-axe and steps towards Bill. Realising he is trapped, Wild Bill turns and draws Lucy and Luck to make one last stand against his corrupted friends.

A fight ensues. Heavily outnumbered, Wild Bill does what he can to fend off the attacks of the infected soldiers and Players. In the training yard, he sees the dead body of Cerlan lying with blood pooling around a stab wound in his torso. From his right, Silas Seer runs towards Wild Bill. His eyes are normal and he has a look of terror on his face.

> *"Wha… what is going on here? Braskin? What are you doing?"*

Wild Bill looks on in shock and horror as Braskin steps towards Silas with axe in hand. Silas implores him to come to his senses, but the goliath cuts a gouge in his chest with a brutal swing. Silas stands dumbfounded, unable to react, and turns to Wild Bill with a look of sorrow and resignation before a second swing of Braskin's axe cleaves his head from his shoulders. His limp body falls to the ground. Wild Bill turns, only to see Caeldrid rushing toward him. First attempting to tackle him to the ground and pass on [[the infection]], Wild Bill is able to resist. However, Caeldrid then draws his sword and runs him through, blood soaking Bill's undershirt and pain shooting through his body.

Wild Bill realises that he is dying. He lies on the cold ground of the training yard floor with his own blood spilling into the dirt. He looks to his side, and sees young Marius Flint locked in a melee with a corrupted Captain Josias. After a short clash of swords, Marius is able to disarm the captain. However, in his shock at his own success, Marius is too slow to react as Josias removes his rifle from his shoulder and slams the butt into Marius' temple. Dazed and bleeding, Marius rushes the captain and stabs him through the belly, covering his hands in Josias' blood. Leaving his sword embedded in his former commander, Marius runs to the Captain's Quarters.

Bill's vision fades as his lifeblood leaves him. In a haze, he sees a brilliant orange light shining in an expanding orb from the centre of the yard. A violet flame appears in a streak around it and swirls, almost as if it is trying to pepper the orb with attacks and smother it. The light grows to blind Wild Bill and he closes his eyes to accept the embrace of death.

Wild Bills eyes blink open. As his vision focuses, he sees the inside of a cell. Around him and across from him, sit his companions. There is no sign of the purple energy in their eyes. From beyond the bars of the cell, Bill sees Captain Josias sitting at the lock-up table, head in his hands. He stands and approaches the Players.

> *"I have come to ask for your help. You are now the only ones that I can trust. One of my men… has been found dead."*
